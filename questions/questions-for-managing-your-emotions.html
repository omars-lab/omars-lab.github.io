What I Ask Myself: When Emotions Take Over

February 11, 2017 · 20 min read
Omar Eid
Principal AI Architect & Entrepreneur

Your emotions are signals, and they are also fuel. The skill is learning which feelings to lean into, which to overcome, and how to stay steady when frustration, fear, or hopelessness show up. The questions below are meant to be sat with, not skimmed. Pick the cluster that matches what you're feeling and let it interrogate you.

Understanding your emotions​

Before I can manage a feeling I have to know what it is and where it came from. These questions name the emotion and find its trigger, which is the start of any real regulation.

Directing your emotions (fuel vs. overcome)​

Not every feeling should be suppressed and not every feeling should be obeyed. These questions sort my emotions into fuel to lean into and weight to overcome.

Controlling your emotions​

Regulation is a skill, not a mood. These questions are about the actual mechanics of influencing my own emotional state, including the hard ones like sorrow.

When you feel frustrated​

Frustration is a signal pointing at something specific. These questions name what's actually frustrating me so I can address the cause instead of stewing in the feeling.

When you feel like giving up​

At the edge of quitting, the right question can tip me back. These questions test whether the hope and the conviction are really gone, or just buried under fatigue.

When you feel like speaking out​

Strong feelings often demand to be voiced, but not every expression helps. These questions check whether speaking out serves a purpose before I let the feeling do the talking.

Conducting others' emotions​

My emotions don't stay inside me; they shape the people around me. These questions ask me to be intentional about the emotional journey I lead others through.

Overcoming fear​

Fear masquerades as caution and shrinks my life one avoidance at a time. These questions confront whether fear is quietly running my days and stopping me from starting.

You are given an opportunity. You can deny it because you're afraid, afraid that you're too young, afraid of the unknown. Or you can take it. This is your choice.

Overcoming negative emotions​

Negative emotions accumulate fastest when I don't track them. These questions and their specific subtypes build a strategy for facing each one head on instead of absorbing them silently.
