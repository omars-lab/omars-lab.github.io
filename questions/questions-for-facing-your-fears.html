What I Ask Myself: When I Face My Fears

February 11, 2017 · 9 min read
Omar Eid
Principal AI Architect & Entrepreneur

Fear rarely announces itself honestly. It hides behind hesitation, busyness, and good excuses. These are the questions I return to when I want to drag a fear into the open and see it clearly, grouped by what they're really asking. Sit with the ones that make you flinch.

Core fears​

Most fear runs in the background, unnamed. These questions force me to say out loud what I'm actually afraid of, because a fear I can name is one I can finally examine.

What threatens you​

Once I know my fears, I look at what's actually behind them. These questions separate real threats from imagined ones so I can spend my courage where it counts.

Overcoming fear​

Naming fear is only half the work. This cluster is about the move from fear to action: what dissolves the fear, what I'd do without it, and how I pressure myself to grow through it.

Courage and faith​

Courage isn't the absence of fear, it's having something stronger to draw on. These questions find the source of my strength so I can reach for it when sight runs out.

Facing opposition​

Opposition, rejection, and setbacks are where fear becomes real and unavoidable. These questions prepare me to meet them on purpose instead of being caught off guard.
