🪪 CLI Terms

The vocabulary I use to organize CLI tooling around roles, skills, and activities, plus the workflows that span them. These nest into each other, so this page looks at them through three lenses:

  1. Glossary — each term defined once.
  2. The model — how they compose, plus the path convention that lays them out.
  3. Distinctions — the contrasts that are easiest to confuse.

1. Glossary​

TermDefinitionThe question it answers
RoleA set of skills and activities that together fulfil a function.Who am I being?
SkillA capability composed of activities and possibly simpler skills.What can I do?
ActivityThe smallest unit of work — atomic, not decomposed further.What's the actual step?
WorkflowMultiple roles working in unison toward a larger outcome.How do roles combine?

2. The model​

Roles, skills, and activities nest by granularity — a role at the top, atomic activities at the bottom, with skills (which can contain sub-skills) in between. Workflows sit above roles, coordinating several at once.

  • A role involves a set of skills and activities.
  • A skill is composed of many different activities and possibly simpler skills.
    • Complex skills break down into simpler skills and activities.
    • Simple skills are just a set of activities.
  • A workflow involves multiple roles working in unison.

Each role and skill has an overview plus the things it contains:

Role  →  /role/overview
           skillset
           activity-set

Skill →  /role/skill/overview
           sub-skills
           activities
           parentSkill

Path convention​

Roles, skills, and activities map onto a directory-like path. Complex activities can nest, and each level has its own overview:

/role/hat
/role/hat/overview
/role/hat/complex-activity/overview
/role/hat/complex-activity/simple-activity
/role/hat/complex-activity/complex-activity
/role/hat/complex-activity/complex-activity/overview
/role/hat/simple-activity

When organizing files, categorize each one individually first, then group them at the end — rather than recursively expanding everything up front.

3. Distinctions (X vs. Y)​

Role vs. Skill — identity vs. capability​

  • A role is who you're being; it bundles many skills and activities.
  • A skill is one capability that role draws on. One role → many skills.

Skill vs. Activity — capability vs. step​

  • A skill can be decomposed (into sub-skills and activities).
  • An activity is atomic — the smallest step, not broken down further.

Complex skill vs. Simple skill — nested vs. flat​

  • A complex skill contains sub-skills as well as activities.
  • A simple skill is just a flat set of activities.

Role vs. Workflow — one actor vs. many​

  • A role is a single actor's bundle of capability.
  • A workflow coordinates multiple roles working in unison.
