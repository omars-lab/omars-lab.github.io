SEO impact of URL renames (route changes are SEO-sensitive)​

Renaming a URL is an SEO-sensitive change. When sections were renamed (Self to Journey, Blog to Thoughts), the old paths (/self/*, /blog/*) were paired with 301 client redirects so links, shares, and search rankings carry over. The open question: what is the actual SEO cost of a rename, and how much of it the redirects recover.

  • Discover the impact of renaming URLs WITHOUT redirects: quantify what breaks if a route is renamed and the old path is left to 404 (lost backlinks, lost ranking, lost Search Console history, broken external/social shares). Establishes why the redirect pairing is non-negotiable.
  • Measure the impact of a rename WITH 301 redirects (the path we took): does ranking fully transfer, is there a temporary dip, how long until the new URL is the canonical one in Google's index? Watch the /self to /journey and /blog to /thoughts to /initiatives renames in Search Console as the live experiment.
    • search.google.com: Search Analytics
  • Audit redirect coverage after any rename: confirm every old permalink 301s to its new home (the createRedirects in docusaurus.config.js handles wildcards; verify no gaps), and submit the updated sitemap so the new URLs are recrawled.
  • Write up the finding as a decision note: a reusable rule for when a URL rename is worth the SEO churn, and the redirect checklist to run every time.

PostHog: person identification & profiles​

Mature the PostHog setup (instrumentation plan: src/posthog-integration-plan.md) beyond anonymous, per-browser events. See PostHog's capturing person profiles docs. Today every visitor is an anonymous distinct_id; these steps tie sessions to people so we can do person-level analysis (return visits, journeys, cohorts).

  • Decide the person-profile mode in src/posthog.js: person_profiles: 'identified_only' (cheaper, privacy-leaning, only creates profiles once identify() is called) vs 'always' (profiles for anonymous users too). Default recommendation: identified_only for a public blog.
  • Call posthog.identify(distinctId, props) at the first meaningful identifying action, e.g. newsletter signup, leaving a comment, or a support click where an email is captured. Anonymous events from before then auto-alias to the identified person.
  • Use posthog.setPersonProperties(set, set_once) for durable traits (first-seen referrer, preferred topics), set_once for values that should never be overwritten.
  • Consider group analytics if a meaningful entity emerges (e.g. company/team for any future gated content), otherwise skip; it adds cost and complexity for a personal blog.
  • Add posthog.reset() on any future logout to avoid binding the next visitor to the prior person.
  • Privacy review (gate): the repo is public and the site is behind a Cloudflare Access bypass app, confirm no PII lands in the public bundle, keep POSTHOG_TEST_MODE out of production (it disables the bot filter), and document the identify triggers in posthog-integration-plan.md.
