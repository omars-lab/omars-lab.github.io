The talks and lines I reach for when I need to pick myself up. Not motivation for its own sake, but
the few messages that actually reset how I carry myself: that I owe the effort to myself, that
knowing who I am not is half the work, and that my job is to build my own vision rather than
optimize someone else's.

<!-- truncate -->

## On owing it to yourself

<SectionBanner why="The talk I put on when I catch myself half-doing the thing I said mattered to me." />

<QuoteSet>

<EditorialQuote source="Matthew McConaughey" cite="from the talk You Owe It To You In 2026" video="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AJ1-WE1B2Ss" reflection="It lands because it moves the debt from the world to me. Nobody else is short-changed when I coast. I am. The clip I keep queued is the turn about two-thirds in, where he stops selling and starts asking.">
You owe it to <Focus>you</Focus>.
</EditorialQuote>

</QuoteSet>

## On knowing who you are not

<SectionBanner why="A reminder that ruling things out is real progress, not indecision." />

<QuoteSet>

<EditorialQuote source="my own note" reflection="It is so much easier to figure out who I am not than who I am, and I used to treat that as a failure. Now I treat each thing I rule out as a real narrowing. Subtraction is direction.">
It is so much easier figuring out who you are <Focus>not</Focus>.
</EditorialQuote>

</QuoteSet>

## On building your own vision

<SectionBanner why="The line I use to check whether I am doing my work or borrowing someone else's." />

<QuoteSet>

<EditorialQuote source="my own affirmation" reflection="I am not someone who brings another person's vision to life, and I am not an optimizer of what already exists. My work is to build my own thing. When a task does not pass that test I feel the drain, and now I take the drain as a signal rather than a mood.">
I bring my <Focus>own</Focus> vision to life.
</EditorialQuote>

</QuoteSet>
