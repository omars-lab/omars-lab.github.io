How a one-line idea ("a central 1 like a Greek pillar, 0s and 1s radiating out into a
pyramid") became a programmatic SVG logo, and the dead ends along the way. Every mark
below is live SVG generated by one small script, so you can flip the background and see
each at true navbar size.

## The idea

The seed: a central **1** that reads like a **Greek pillar**. Flanking it, mirrored
outward from the center, come **0**s, then **1**s, then **0**s, and so on. Each column
is a vertical stack of its digit, the stacks get shorter and the glyphs smaller as they
move away from center, and they share a baseline. The result is a **pyramid built out of
1s and 0s**: binary, and monumental.

Everything is produced by a parameterized generator (a Node script that emits SVG paths),
so each design below is just a different config object. That let us explore the space fast
instead of hand-drawing each option.

## First attempt: stacked glyphs

The first cut drew each column as a literal stack of digit glyphs: a styled **1** glyph
and a ring-shaped **0** glyph, repeated up the column. It made a clean pyramid, and the
**1**s carried a capital + base serif so they hinted at columns.

<SvgVariantGrid variants={VARIANTS} group="journey" previewHeights={[32]} />

The problem showed up the moment we tried to make the **1**s genuinely *Ionic*. Stacking
many small column-glyphs on top of each other did not read as one Ionic column. It read
as a totem of stacked capitals. The architecture was fighting the construction: a real
column is **one shaft with one capital and one base**, not a repeated motif.

## The fix: real architectural columns

So we changed the model. In "arch" mode, each **1** column is drawn as **one real
column** spanning the whole stack height: a capital on top, a single tall fluted shaft,
and a base at the bottom. The **0** columns stay as stacked rings. Two classical orders:

- **Doric**: a plain flared capital, no scrolls. Clean and sturdy.
- **Ionic**: the scrolled **volute** capital, drawn from a reference column SVG.

<SvgVariantGrid variants={VARIANTS} group="elongated" pillarBackdrop darkToggle={false} />

Getting the **Ionic capital** right took the most iteration. Early versions put the flat
abacus bar *below* the scrolls and drew them as two detached rings (they looked like
handles). The fix, guided by a reference column: the **stepped bar sits on top** (mirrored
from the foot), the **volutes are large and scroll inward** just beneath it, and the
shaft is stocky enough to carry real column weight. The **spiral** volute reads as a true
Ionic scroll; the **curl** (a solid ring) still reads as a handle.

## Stacked drums

A real ancient column was assembled from stacked stone **drums**. So we also tried
building each **1** from several short columns stacked, capital-and-base bands repeating up
the height. It is denser and more textured, an "ancient ruins" feel. Adding a small
vertical gap between drums let them breathe.

## Arrangement experiments

The pyramid is one arrangement, not the only one. The same column and ring glyphs can be
laid out very differently:

- **All same height**: a uniform colonnade, a temple facade rather than a pyramid.
- **No zeros**: drop the ring columns entirely for a pure pyramid of columns. The cleanest
  and most monumental of all the options.
- **Wedge / diverging V**: a perspective arrangement. From a close central "footsteps"
  point, two rows of columns radiate up and outward into the distance, suggesting an
  endless, far-reaching colonnade.

<SvgVariantGrid variants={VARIANTS} group="arrange" previewHeights={[32]} />

## Perspective: the wanderer's vantage

The wedge hinted at depth, so we pushed it into true perspective: place the viewer on the
ground, like a wanderer standing among ruins, and let the columns recede. Two vantage
points fell out of that:

- **Ground / sideways diverging-V**: stand facing the central pillar (nearest and
  tallest). Rows of columns recede outward to **both** the left and right, shrinking and
  rising toward a horizon on each side. Vast and far-reaching.
- **Avenue / straight-back aisle**: stand at the head of a single colonnade. Two rows of
  columns march **straight away** from you to one vanishing point, near columns large in
  front, far ones small and converging. Like walking into a temple.

<SvgVariantGrid variants={VARIANTS} group="perspective" previewHeights={[32]} />

## Gold: interlaced bars and zeros

A different way to keep the **binary** meaning explicit: instead of separate **0** columns
between the pillars, put the binary **inside** each column. The shaft **interlaces** solid
vertical **bars** with vertical **chains of 0s** that fill the gaps between them, and the
left and right **edges stay solid** so the column still reads as a column. Two tones of
gold (brighter bars, deeper 0s) make the interlace legible. The form carries the **1**, the
grooves carry the **0**s, and the whole thing reads like a golden temple. Both Ionic and
Doric work.

<SvgVariantGrid variants={VARIANTS} group="gold" previewHeights={[32]} />

## A place to stand

The interlaced gold columns were given a setting: a **sleek backdrop** rather than empty
space. Just a single two-tone vertical gradient, kept deliberately flat and minimal so the
gold stays the focus (an earlier sun-glow version was dropped for being too busy). Three
moods: desert, dusk, night.

## What worked, what did not

| Decision | Verdict |
|---|---|
| Stack small glyphs to fake a column | Did not work (reads as a totem of capitals) |
| One real column per **1** (arch mode) | Worked (genuine Doric / Ionic columns) |
| Curl volute (solid ring) | Reads as a handle, not a scroll |
| Spiral volute, abacus on top | Worked (true Ionic capital) |
| Stacked drums with a gap | Worked (rich "ruins" texture) |
| No-zeros column pyramid | Cleanest, strongest single mark |
| Wedge perspective | Evocative and vast, but wide aspect (hero, not icon) |
| Ground / avenue perspective | Cinematic depth; wide or tall, best as a hero banner |
| Gold shafts as chains of 0s | Keeps the binary meaning literal; striking on dark |
| Mixed 1/0 bits in the shaft | Reads as actual code, not just zeros |
| Temple/desert scene backdrop | Gives the columns a place to stand; most evocative |

## The open question: navbar legibility

Every mark above is also rendered at **32px**, the height it would actually be next to the
site title. That row is the honest test, and it is unforgiving: the full pyramid, and
especially the fine spiral volutes and flutes, blur into hash at navbar size. The detail
that makes the mark beautiful large is exactly what disappears small.

The likely resolution is a **two-tier** identity: a detailed column arrangement as the
**hero** mark (welcome page, social card, this kind of post), paired with a **simplified
reduction** for the navbar and favicon. That decision, and the final pick among the
arrangements above, is still open.
