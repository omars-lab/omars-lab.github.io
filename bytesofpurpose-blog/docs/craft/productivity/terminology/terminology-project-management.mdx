The verbs I use to talk about project management, **planning, managing,
organizing, distilling**, plus the artifact they revolve around, the **plan**.
They overlap in everyday speech, so this page looks at them through three lenses:

1. **[Glossary](#1-glossary)**: each term defined once.
2. **[How they fit together](#2-how-they-fit-together)**: what acts on what.
3. **[Distinctions](#3-distinctions-x-vs-y)**: the contrasts that trip me up.

## 1. Glossary

| Term | Definition | The question it answers |
|---|---|---|
| **Planning** | Determining all the tasks needed to achieve a goal; assembling a plan of action. | *What needs to be done?* |
| **Managing** | Continuously deciding between competing demands on the same resource (time, attention). | *Given everything in flight, what gives?* |
| **Organizing** | Arranging existing things into a usable structure. | *Where does each thing belong?* |
| **Distilling** | Reducing a sprawl of material down to its essentials. | *What actually matters here?* |
| **Plan** | The artifact planning produces, a stated intent + the tasks to realize it. | *What's the agreed path?* |

## 2. How they fit together

Planning is something I do *across* all my habits and buckets, it isn't a place
of its own. So rather than a single "planning" folder, planning-oriented activity
lives wherever the work lives, and I link to it.

- **Plans revolve around initiatives.** Saying "I plan to do X" is intent;
  planning *what to do to realize X* is the [initiative](./terminology-development)
  itself.
- **Plans vary in cadence.** Some repeat year over year; others are ad hoc.
- **Priorities are managed, not planned.** You plan a goal's tasks, but when many
  things compete for the same time, that's a *management* problem, not a planning
  one (see the distinction below).

## 3. Distinctions (X vs. Y)

### Planning vs. Managing, *lay out the path vs. referee the conflicts*
- **Planning** determines the tasks to reach a goal, it's forward-looking
  composition.
- **Managing** kicks in when commitments collide: many habits or tasks fighting
  for the same time. You don't "plan" priorities; you **manage** them.
- Reference: [Google: "does one plan priorities or manage them"](https://www.google.com/search?q=does+one+plan+priorities+or+manage+them)

### Planning vs. Organizing, *create the path vs. arrange what exists*
- **Planning** produces a new path toward a goal.
- **Organizing** rearranges things that already exist into a usable structure.

### Plan (intent) vs. Initiative (realization)
- A **plan** can be a stated intent, "I plan to do X eventually."
- Working out *what to do to realize X* is an **initiative**, a different thing.
  See [Development Terms](./terminology-development).
