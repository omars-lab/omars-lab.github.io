# Ideas

This is the raw front of the idea-to-ship lifecycle: where a rough thought is captured before
it is groomed into something real. An idea here is a candidate, not a commitment. The good ones
graduate into initiatives, then projects, and once shipped and reflected on, the durable lesson
is distilled back into the relevant Craft topic.

## How an idea matures

A captured idea is usually under-specified: the motivation lives in my head, the value is
assumed, and "done" is undefined. Maturing it means answering, in order: **why** (the
motivation), **what it's worth** (the value and the cost of not doing it), **how far** (the
scope), **the steps** (a real to-do list), and **how I'll know it worked** (success criteria).
Only then is it ready to act on. The `mature-content` skill runs that interview; once an idea is
board-ready, `groom-initiatives` advances it across the board.

## The Ideas board
