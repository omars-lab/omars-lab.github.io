# Ideas

This is the raw front of the idea-to-ship lifecycle: where a rough thought is captured before
it is groomed into something real. An idea here is a candidate, not a commitment.

## Actioned vs. unactioned: where an idea lives

The dividing line is whether I have **acted on** the idea, not whether it is finished:

- An idea I have **had but not acted on** is a post on the **[Thoughts & Ideas](/thoughts)**
  blog. These are the unmaterialized ones: a "should I…", a "what would it take…", a captured
  backlog item.
- The moment I **start acting on it**, it graduates out of Thoughts & Ideas and becomes a dated
  **[Initiative](/initiatives)** (an experiment, a project log, a real attempt). Once shipped and
  reflected on, the durable lesson is distilled back into the relevant Craft topic.

This page is the **durable** half: the board that indexes the unactioned ideas and the framework
for maturing them. The idea **posts themselves** are temporal, so they live on the
[Thoughts & Ideas](/thoughts) blog. It is exactly the same split the
[Experiments](/product-management/experiments) page uses, where the durable board lives here in
Craft and the dated experiment posts live on a blog.

## The Ideas board

Ideas appear here as cards, generated from the frontmatter of the
[Thoughts & Ideas](/thoughts) posts that carry `board: ideas`. A card's column is its `stage`,
read straight from the post, so the board can never drift from the posts it indexes. Click a card
to open the idea.

<KanbanBoard board="ideas" />

| Column | What it means |
|---|---|
| Backlog | Captured, still raw, not yet groomed. |
| Exploring | Being matured: motivation, value, scope, and a plan are taking shape. |
| Building | I have started acting on it (at this point it usually also becomes an Initiative). |
| Shipped | Done; the durable lesson is ready to distill into Craft. |

## The first-time (🌱) class

Some cards carry a **🌱 First-time** badge. This is a *classification* of a subset of the ideas,
not a column: the ones about doing something for the **first time**, or trying a new technology
hands-on. I used to call these my "Hello Worlds." An idea is first-time when:

- it is about doing something for the FIRST time (my first iOS app, my first plugin);
- it is exploratory: trying a new technology, framework, or platform to learn it hands-on;
- it is unactioned: a thing I have wanted to try, not yet started.

The badge comes from the post's `class: first-time` frontmatter, so the first-time ideas surface
right on the board above (and filter by the `first-time` tag chip) instead of needing a separate
list.

## How an idea matures

A captured idea is usually under-specified: the motivation lives in my head, the value is
assumed, and "done" is undefined. Maturing it means answering, in order: **why** (the
motivation), **what it's worth** (the value and the cost of not doing it), **how far** (the
scope), **the steps** (a real to-do list), and **how I'll know it worked** (success criteria).
Only then is it ready to act on. The `mature-content` skill runs that interview; once an idea is
board-ready, `groom-initiatives` advances it across the board.

## Where the ideas live

The captured, still-unactioned ideas are posts on the **[Thoughts & Ideas](/thoughts)** blog.
Each idea is its own post, and the board above is the live index: every card is one idea, and the
**tag chips** on a card let you browse by theme (scripting, automation, notes, backup, app, and so
on). Filter the board by a tag to see just that family of ideas.
