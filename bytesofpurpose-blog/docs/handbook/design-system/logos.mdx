# Logos and Iconography

The brand favors **simple, theme-aware marks** and **text glyphs** over a heavy icon font. Two
monochrome logos, a small set of line-style feature icons, a handful of purposeful glyphs, and the
hand-drawn arch illustrations carry the visual identity.

## Brand marks

Two theme-aware monochrome marks that inherit their color from the surrounding text. Toggle the
navbar theme to see them adapt.

<BrandMarks />

- The **lightbulb `</>`** mark is the primary logo: a bulb with a code glyph and radiating rays.
- The **binary-pyramid** mark is the secondary or favicon mark: columns of 1 and 0 digits radiating
  from a central pillar. It is generated from a config in the source repo; the SVG is the artifact.

## Feature icons

A small set of line-style SVGs used on the homepage feature cards, tinted brand-green. They are the
only bundled icon set; when a UI needs a broader library, substitute **Lucide** (a thin, rounded
line style that matches these) and flag the substitution.

## Glyphs and emoji

The brand reaches for **text glyphs** as icons before an icon font:

- **`→`** on a "Read more" link.
- **`★`** on premium surfaces.
- **`☕`** the "Buy Me a Coffee?" cup in the navbar.
- **`·`** the middot, the brand's connective separator in eyebrows, metadata, and footers.

**Emoji** appear only as the single leading glyph on a navigation card or a section header, never
mid-sentence and never as decorative clutter. On this blog, the leading emoji on a post title also
encodes the **kind** of post; the full legend lives in the [Handbook](/handbook).

## Arch illustrations

Imagery is the hand-drawn **warm cartoon** set: cozy brown-and-green scenes clipped into a
**cathedral-arch silhouette** (`border-radius: 110px 110px 14px 14px`) with an outlined edge and a
soft drop-shadow. Warm, narrative, personal, never stock photography and never gradient abstraction.

<ArchIllustrations />

To prepare a new scene so it abides by the canonical arch and drops in cleanly, see the
`import-arched-image` workflow in the repo.
