# Buttons

Buttons are the brand's action controls: any click, submit, or call-to-action. They are rendered
here **live from the site's real button classes and tokens**, so what you see is what ships (toggle
light and dark in the navbar to confirm). Hover darkens or lifts them, respecting the brand's quiet
motion.

## Variants

Four variants, each for a different weight of action.

<ButtonRow>
  <DemoButton variant="primary">Read the blog</DemoButton>
  <DemoButton variant="secondary">Browse docs</DemoButton>
  <DemoButton variant="ghost">Read more</DemoButton>
  <DemoButton variant="premium" iconLeft="★">Unlock premium</DemoButton>
</ButtonRow>

- **Primary** · filled forest-green with white ink (auto-swapped to dark ink in dark mode for AA).
  The main action on a surface.
- **Secondary** · a green outline. A secondary action next to a primary one.
- **Ghost** · text-only with a tinted hover. The quietest action, for inline or repeated links.
- **Premium** · a gold wash for locked or premium calls-to-action.

:::note On the premium variant
The repo ships the green primary, secondary, and ghost buttons as Infima classes. It has the
`--premium-gold*` palette (used on the premium gate and lock badges) but no ready-made premium
*button* class, so the premium button above is composed from those real gold tokens. It matches
the brand; it just is not yet a standing button class.
:::

## Sizes

Three sizes: `sm`, `md` (default), and `lg`.

<ButtonRow>
  <DemoButton variant="primary" size="sm">Small</DemoButton>
  <DemoButton variant="primary" size="md">Medium</DemoButton>
  <DemoButton variant="primary" size="lg">Large</DemoButton>
</ButtonRow>

## Icon slots

A button can carry a leading or trailing glyph. The brand uses simple text glyphs (`→`, `★`) rather
than an icon font.

<ButtonRow>
  <DemoButton variant="ghost" iconRight="→">Read more</DemoButton>
  <DemoButton variant="primary" iconLeft="☕">Buy me a coffee</DemoButton>
  <DemoButton variant="premium" iconLeft="★">Premium</DemoButton>
</ButtonRow>

## States and motion

Hover darkens the fill (primary) or tints the surface (secondary, ghost), with a small
`--lift-subtle` translate. There is no special press scale. All of it respects
`prefers-reduced-motion` (the lift holds still when reduced-motion is on). The radius is
`--radius-sm` (`--ifm-button-border-radius`, `0.4rem`) on every button.
