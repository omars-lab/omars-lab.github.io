# 🪪 Companies Terminology

Shared language for talking about roles, skills, and culture inside companies. This
glossary is a starting point that grows alongside the [Companies](/craft/companies)
essays.

## Roles & Levels

- **Level**: a company's banding of scope, autonomy, and impact (e.g. SDE I/II/III,
  Senior, Staff). Expectations rise with each level. See
  [Understanding SDE Levels](/knowledge/career/understanding-differences-in-skills).
- **Scope**: the breadth of problem an engineer owns, from a task to a feature to a
  system to an organization-wide concern.
- **Autonomy**: how much direction someone needs; higher levels operate with less.
- **Impact**: the measurable effect of the work on users, the team, or the business.

## Competencies / Skills

- **Technical skills**: the engineering abilities evaluated in interviews and on the
  job. See [Technical Skills Evaluation](/knowledge/career/understanding-desireable-tech-skills).
- **Leadership principles**: the behavioral bar companies hire and promote against.
  See [Leadership Principles Companies Look For](/knowledge/career/understanding-desireable-leadership-skills).
- **Soft skills**: communication, collaboration, and judgment under ambiguity. See
  [Soft Skills Evaluation](/knowledge/career/understanding-desireable-soft-skills).

## Culture

- **Company culture**: the shared values and norms that shape how a company operates;
  often varies with company size and stage. See
  [Understanding Tech Company Culture](/knowledge/career/2025-09-25-understanding-cultural-values).
- **Values**: the explicit principles a company states and (ideally) rewards. See
  [Understanding Zapier Values](/knowledge/career/2025-09-25-understanding-zapier-values)
  for a worked example.
