The vocabulary I use to organize personal development — software, crafts, or
self-improvement. Five terms do most of the work: **goals, initiatives,
projects, ideas, and priorities**. They're easy to blur together, so this page
defines each one and then looks at them through three lenses:

1. **[Glossary](#1-glossary)** — each term defined once, with the question it answers.
2. **[The hierarchy](#2-the-hierarchy-high--low)** — how they stack from high-level intent down to concrete work.
3. **[Distinctions](#3-distinctions-x-vs-y)** — the pairwise "X vs. Y" contrasts that are easiest to confuse.

Pick whichever lens fits how you're thinking; together they should build a full
picture.

## 1. Glossary

| Term | Definition | The question it answers |
|---|---|---|
| **Goal** | An outcome I want to reach. | *What am I aiming for?* |
| **Initiative** | A high-level effort I take on to achieve a goal. Carries an element of goal-setting — it's a desire to build something. | *What do I want to pursue?* |
| **Project** | A scoped, concrete unit of work. | *What am I building?* |
| **Idea** | A candidate thing I could do — lives on a backlog until acted on. | *What could I do?* |
| **Priority** | Something I need to do **now**, pulled from the active set. | *What do I do next?* |

## 2. The hierarchy (high → low)

Goals, initiatives, and projects form a ladder of *altitude* — from intent down
to concrete work. Ideas and priorities cut across that ladder: ideas feed it,
priorities pull from it.

```
Goal          the outcome I want
 │ pursued via
Initiative    a high-level effort toward the goal  ──generates──▶  Ideas
 │ broken into                                                      (backlog)
Project       scoped, concrete work                                   │
                                                                  pull "now"
                                                                      ▼
                                                                  Priorities
```

- **Goals → Initiatives → Projects** is the top-down decomposition: a goal is
  realized through one or more initiatives, each of which breaks down into
  projects.
- **Initiatives generate ideas.** As I engage with an initiative, it recursively
  spins off a set of new ideas to enhance it — building the thing is itself an
  idea, and going deeper produces more.
- **Priorities are a selection, not a level.** They're whatever I've pulled out
  of the active set to work on right now.

## 3. Distinctions (X vs. Y)

The contrasts that are easiest to confuse, each referring back to the single
definition above.

### Initiatives vs. Priorities — *want vs. need-now*
- **Initiatives** are things I **want** to do.
- **Priorities** are things I **need** to do **now**.

### Initiatives vs. Projects — *goal-bearing vs. scoped*
- **Initiatives** encode goals I have for myself; I prefer the word because it
  carries that goal-setting element.
- **Projects** are the scoped efforts you complete to achieve the goal.
- Reference: [What are product goals and initiatives? (Aha!)](https://www.aha.io/roadmapping/guide/product-strategy/what-are-product-goals-and-initiatives)

### Goals vs. Initiatives — *outcome vs. effort*
- A **goal** is the outcome you want; an **initiative** is the effort you pursue
  to reach it. They're a matched pair.
- Reference: [Aha! — Goals and initiatives](https://www.aha.io/roadmapping/guide/product-strategy/what-are-product-goals-and-initiatives#:~:text=Goals%20and%20initiatives%20are%20a,pursue%20to%20reach%20those%20goals.)

### Ideas vs. Initiatives — *backlog vs. committed effort*
- An **idea** is a candidate; an **initiative** is something I've committed to.
- Initiatives *generate* ideas recursively, and those ideas split into:
  - **Actionable ideas** — things I can act on now.
  - **Future ideas** — things I can eventually do.

### Ideas vs. Initiatives vs. Priorities — *the working flow*
- I keep a **board of ideas** — things I *could* do.
- I promote some to **initiatives** I've engaged with.
- I pull a few into **priorities** I'm working on right now.
