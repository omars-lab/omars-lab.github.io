A working glossary of the terms I use to organize personal development — whether
software, crafts, or self-improvement — and how they relate to one another.

## Initiatives vs. Priorities

- **Initiatives:** Things I **want** to do — *What do I want to do?*
- **Priorities:** Things I **need** to do **now** — *What do I need to do now?*

## Initiatives vs. Projects

- Initiatives encode goals I have for myself. I tend to use "initiative" more
  than "project" because it carries an element of goal setting.
- Initiatives are high-level efforts you complete in order to achieve each goal.
  - Reference: [What are product goals and initiatives? (Aha!)](https://www.aha.io/roadmapping/guide/product-strategy/what-are-product-goals-and-initiatives)

## Goals vs. Initiatives

Goals and initiatives are a pair: goals are the outcomes you want, and
initiatives are the efforts you pursue to reach those goals.

- Reference: [Aha! — Goals and initiatives](https://www.aha.io/roadmapping/guide/product-strategy/what-are-product-goals-and-initiatives#:~:text=Goals%20and%20initiatives%20are%20a,pursue%20to%20reach%20those%20goals.)

## Ideas vs. Initiatives

- An initiative will generate a recurring set of new ideas to enhance the
  initiative.
- An initiative is almost a desire to build something new — where building it is
  itself an idea.
- As I dive deeper into an initiative, I recursively develop new ideas.

Regarding any initiative, ideas fall into two buckets:

- **Actionable ideas** — things I can act on now.
- **Future ideas** — things I can eventually do.

## Ideas vs. Initiatives vs. Priorities

- I have a **board of ideas** — things I *could* do.
- I have **initiatives** I have engaged with.
- I have **priorities** I am working on now.
