This is where I keep the **sets of questions I ask**. Some are introspective: the questions I ask
myself to understand who I am and what I want. Some are practical: the questions worth sitting with
before starting a business, a blog, or anything that matters. They are important enough to be their
own collection, distinct from the ideas that merely [occurred to me](/thoughts) and the inputs I
[keep to shape my thinking](/mindset).

<!-- truncate -->

## A question is a flashlight

A good question does not answer anything for you; it points at the part of the room you have been
avoiding. I keep these grouped by what they are really about, revisit a set when its season comes
around, and write the answer down so I can watch it move over the years. The practice and the legend
behind the introspective sets live in [What I Ask Myself](/handbook/what-i-ask-myself).

## Two kinds of question set

- **Introspective:** questions I ask *myself*, to shape who I am. About purpose, fear, worth,
  inaction, what I want. The "What I Ask Myself" series.
- **Practical:** questions worth asking before *doing* something. Starting a business, starting a
  blog, deciding what to learn. The "Questions for…" series.
