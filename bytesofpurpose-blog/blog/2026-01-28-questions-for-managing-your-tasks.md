Tasks are the atoms of your work: the individual things that make up your plans and initiatives. But how you capture, track, and structure them determines whether your system actually serves you or just collects clutter. The questions below are prompts to interrogate your own task process: how tasks get in, how they move, what they depend on, and what could be automated.

<!-- truncate -->

## Capturing tasks

- What should I be able to do through my task-tracking process?
- How do I capture tasks and things that need to be done?
- What is my personal model of tasks?
- What tools do I need to better manage my tasks?
- Do I only capture tasks as "a planner"? Is planning the main reason tasks get captured?

## Tracking tasks

- What tools do I use to track what I have to do?
- How do I track my progress?
- How do I figure out what I should be working on today?
- How do I assign myself a new task to do?
- How will I better manage and maintain my tasks over time?
- What is my tagging method? If I tag something as a "thought," I want to see all the thoughts I've had scattered across the days.
- Could I auto-derive all my tag lists scattered across the days?
- For things I append to a tag list, how long does it take me to complete them?
- How can I improve my task-tracking process, and what ideas have I had to improve it?

### Immediate tasks

- What do I need to do right now?
- What is my immediate task list?
- What is the next action for each project?

### Desire to do

- What do I want to do but haven't captured as a task?
- What are all the different things I want to do?
- When did I want to do these different things?
- Did I ever stop wanting to do something? Why?

## Capturing triggers

- What triggers or events lead to me needing to do something?
- Could I have trigger-based reminders, such as when a store has a sale, when I get my paycheck, when I arrive somewhere, when I check my phone, or when I go too long without doing something?

## Recurring tasks

- What recurring tasks do I have?
- Could a script recreate tasks based on hashtags like a recurring "weekly" marker?

## Predicting potential tasks

- How do I predict potential tasks?
- Could task tracking auto-detect recurring tasks from similar wordings and predict when I'll have to do the task next?
- Could I have dynamic dates — instead of specifying an exact date, I specify something like "NextFinancialReview" and the task appears on the appropriate day, with configurable frequencies per dynamic date and the ability to postpone to the next occurrence?
- Could I specify a date range for a goal (a start and end date) so the goal applies for the whole period and shows up on every day in that span?

## Task dependencies

- How do I declare that some tasks cannot be started until other events finish?
- What tasks depend on other tasks?
- What is blocking my tasks?
- In what order should tasks be done?
- What triggers a task to become active and ready to be worked on?
- How do I represent dependencies between tasks and events?
- What is the criteria for beginning work on a task?
- How do I declare task dependencies, including tasks that depend on events occurring and tasks that depend on other tasks — e.g. when task A *completes* start task B, or when task A *starts* start task B?
- What else can a task depend on?
- Could I extract a list of events from these kinds of todos, and template-ize event annotations (like "beforeEarningX")? Could I auto-add a due date as soon as the `@when` condition is met?

## Automating tasks

- What task creation can be automated?
- What repetitive task management can be scripted?
- How can I automate reminders and recurring tasks?
- Should I create todos for the number of stashed commits I have (implying a backlog, since it hasn't been committed)?
- Should I create a todo for things I want to buy when I take a picture of a product?
- Should I create a todo for things I want to buy when I add an item to my Amazon cart?

## Breaking down work

- What is the advantage of using stories over tasks? Reduced number of tickets?
- Why (as a company) are we working on this feature?
- What needs to be done to accomplish this feature, and in what order?
- Which resources should I allocate to perform a specific task?

## Structuring your tasks

*Note: If weight >= 1, the task is repetitive — it should happen around once a week.*

The structure of my tasks is as follows:

```
Goal A
     Project A for Goal A
          Research A for Project A
          Task A for Project A
              Subtask A for Task A
         Subtask B for Project A
     Task B for Goal A
     Research B for Goal A
    Subtask C for Goal A
```
