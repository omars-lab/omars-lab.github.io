There is always more to learn than time to learn it. The hard part is not finding material, it is deciding what deserves your attention next and then actually committing to it. These questions walk from picking topics, to prioritizing them, to building a curriculum, to protecting the time and keeping the commitments.

<!-- truncate -->

## What to learn

- What do I want to learn? What should I be learning?
- What should I master in, and what is valuable for me to learn?
- What are my strengths and weaknesses?
- What do I want to accomplish in my life, and what learning does that imply?
- Where do I want to grow?
- What bundles of knowledge do I want to learn more about?
- What do I want to study on the side?
- Should I make a learning catalogue of everything I want to learn?

## Learning priorities

- What are my learning priorities?
- Which topics have I done before, so I can estimate their level of difficulty?
- What is most valuable for me to learn first?

## Your learning plan and curriculum

- What is my learning plan? What will I learn, and by when?
- What is my learning journey and curriculum?
- What learning goals will I set for myself?
- What do I think is worth learning, and what should I focus on now?
- What will the phases of my learning be?
- What is my learning backlog of things I could possibly learn?
- Which items from my backlog have I actually committed myself to learning?

## Making time for learning

- How do I maximize how much time I allocate to learning?
- Could I learn during idle time, the way some people listen to audiobooks while exercising, vacuuming, doing the dishes, or grilling?
- What time have I explicitly allocated for learning?
- Can I learn from any material while exercising, or is there a specific kind that sticks better?

## Learning commitments

- How will I commit myself to learning, and what exactly have I committed to learn?
- What resources have I committed to working through?
- How much time am I going to spend learning every week? Could I aim for something like 4 hours per week of studying, reading, and programming?
- Am I keeping up with my commitments?
- Should I be working on any certifications? Why are certifications important?
- Are certifications the only milestone that can reflect my level of learning?

## Overlapping learning with work

- How might my learning plan shift based on the opportunities I have to work on different technologies at work?
- Where can I deliberately overlap what I am learning with what I am building?

## Consolidating your learning material

- Where do I capture notes on all the different things I want to learn from?
- How do I index all of my learning material in one place?
- Could I categorize my browser bookmarks, consolidate saved links from LinkedIn, gather the photos of books on my phone, and collect screenshots of useful websites?

## Feeding your curiosity

- What am I curious about right now, and what does that push me to learn more about?
- What are the different kinds of things I want to learn?
