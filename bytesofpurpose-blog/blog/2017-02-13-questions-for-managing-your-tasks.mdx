Tasks are the atoms of my work: the individual things that make up my plans and initiatives. But how I capture, track, and structure them determines whether my system actually serves me or just collects clutter. The questions below are prompts to interrogate my own task process: how tasks get in, how they move, what they depend on, and what could be automated.

<!-- truncate -->

## Capturing tasks

<SectionBanner why="A task system is only as good as what makes it in. If capture is hard or unclear, half my work never reaches the list and lives in my head instead." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  priority="high"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="moderate"
  why="If I'm fuzzy on what my process is even supposed to do, I'll keep bolting on tools that don't fit the actual job."
  howOften="When setting up or overhauling my task system"
  when="Before adopting a new tool or workflow"
  record="A short spec of what my task process must let me do">
  What should I be able to do through my task-tracking process? How do I capture tasks and things that need to be done?
</Question>

<Question
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Without a clear model of what a 'task' even is to me, my list ends up mixing thoughts, errands, and goals into mush."
  howOften="When my list feels chaotic or hard to scan"
  when="During a cleanup of my task system"
  record="Write down my model of a task and what tools it needs">
  What is my personal model of tasks? What tools do I need to better manage my tasks?
</Question>

<Question
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="moderate"
  why="If I only capture tasks when I'm in planning mode, everything I think of in the moment slips away. Worth checking why capture happens at all."
  howOften="Occasionally, when reviewing how things enter my system"
  when="When I notice tasks only appear during dedicated planning"
  record="Note what actually triggers me to capture a task">
  Do I only capture tasks as "a planner"? Is planning the main reason tasks get captured?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Tracking tasks

<SectionBanner why="Capture gets tasks in; tracking is how I know where each one stands and what to touch next. Without it, the list rots into a pile I avoid." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  cron="weekly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Tracking tools and a clear progress signal are what let me trust the list instead of re-deriving my workload from memory each day."
  howOften="Weekly, as part of reviewing the system"
  when="During a weekly review of progress"
  record="Note the tools I use and how I read progress at a glance">
  What tools do I use to track what I have to do? How do I track my progress?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="high"
  cron="daily"
  depth="quick"
  why="The daily question is the one that matters most: from everything on the list, what should I actually pick up today?"
  howOften="Daily, when starting work"
  when="At the start of each day, and when assigning myself something new"
  record="A simple way to surface today's candidates from the full list">
  How do I figure out what I should be working on today? How do I assign myself a new task to do?
</Question>

<Question
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="A task system drifts toward clutter unless I actively maintain it. Asking how to keep it healthy is what prevents bankruptcy of the list."
  howOften="Monthly, as a maintenance pass"
  when="During a monthly review of the system itself"
  record="Capture the maintenance habits that keep the list usable over time">
  How will I better manage and maintain my tasks over time?
</Question>

<Question
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="moderate"
  why="A consistent tagging method is what lets me pull together scattered items, like every 'thought' I've had across many days, into one view."
  howOften="When refining how I categorize and retrieve tasks"
  when="When I want a cross-cutting view of one kind of item"
  record="Define my tags and how I'd query across them">
  What is my tagging method? If I tag something as a "thought," I want to see all the thoughts I've had scattered across the days. Could I auto-derive all my tag lists scattered across the days?
</Question>

<Question
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Knowing how long tagged items take to finish tells me which kinds of work I underestimate, and where my process keeps leaking time."
  howOften="Monthly, when looking for ways to improve the process"
  when="During a retrospective on how the system is serving me"
  record="Track completion time per tag list, and log improvement ideas as they come">
  For things I append to a tag list, how long does it take me to complete them? How can I improve my task-tracking process, and what ideas have I had to improve it?
</Question>

### Immediate tasks

<SectionBanner why="Underneath tracking sits the now: the small set I can act on this instant. These keep me anchored to the next concrete action instead of the whole pile." />

<Question
  priority="high"
  cron="daily"
  depth="quick"
  why="Most stuckness comes from staring at everything at once. Narrowing to the immediate list and the next action per project is what unblocks me."
  howOften="Daily, and whenever I feel stuck"
  when="At the start of a work session"
  record="A short immediate list with the next action named for each project">
  What do I need to do right now? What is my immediate task list? What is the next action for each project?
</Question>

### Desire to do

<SectionBanner why="Not everything I want to do has earned a task yet. Surfacing those wants keeps the wishlist honest and shows me when a desire has quietly died." />

<Question
  cron="weekly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="The gap between what I want to do and what I've captured is where good ideas go to be forgotten. Naming it brings them back into view."
  howOften="Weekly, as a sweep of uncaptured wants"
  when="During a weekly review of my backlog"
  record="A list of wants not yet turned into tasks, with when each one started">
  What do I want to do but haven't captured as a task? What are all the different things I want to do? When did I want to do these different things?
</Question>

<Question
  power="spark"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="When a desire fades, that's worth noticing. The reason I stopped wanting something often reveals what actually matters to me now."
  howOften="Monthly, when pruning the wishlist"
  when="When I catch myself no longer wanting something I once did"
  record="Note what I stopped wanting and why">
  Did I ever stop wanting to do something? Why?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Capturing triggers

<SectionBanner why="Some tasks shouldn't live on a fixed date but should fire when something happens in the world. These questions are about catching those event-driven needs." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Trigger-based reminders match a task to the moment it's actually relevant, which beats a calendar date I'll ignore when the context is wrong."
  howOften="When designing reminders, or when a date-based one keeps failing"
  when="When I notice a task really belongs to an event, not a date"
  record="List the events and conditions that should spawn a task">
  What triggers or events lead to me needing to do something? Could I have trigger-based reminders, such as when a store has a sale, when I get my paycheck, when I arrive somewhere, when I check my phone, or when I go too long without doing something?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Recurring tasks

<SectionBanner why="A surprising share of my work repeats. Naming the recurring tasks (and dreaming up ways to regenerate them) keeps me from re-entering the same things forever." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  cron="weekly"
  depth="quick"
  why="Recurring tasks are the backbone of a routine. If I don't name them, I either forget them or waste effort recreating them by hand each cycle."
  howOften="Weekly, when the recurring set comes due"
  when="During a weekly review of routines"
  record="A list of recurring tasks, plus ideas for scripting their recreation">
  What recurring tasks do I have? Could a script recreate tasks based on hashtags like a recurring "weekly" marker?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Predicting potential tasks

<SectionBanner why="Beyond what already recurs, there's work I can see coming. These questions explore predicting tasks before they're due so they appear at the right moment on their own." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power="spark"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="deep"
  why="If my system could spot recurring patterns from how I phrase things and predict the next occurrence, I'd offload the remembering entirely."
  howOften="When thinking about how to make the system smarter"
  when="During design work on the task tooling itself"
  record="Sketch how auto-detection of recurring tasks would work">
  How do I predict potential tasks? Could task tracking auto-detect recurring tasks from similar wordings and predict when I'll have to do the task next?
</Question>

<Question
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="deep"
  why="A named, dynamic date (like 'NextFinancialReview') is more durable than a hard-coded one: it always lands on the right day even as the schedule shifts."
  howOften="When designing scheduling features"
  when="When a fixed date keeps needing manual adjustment"
  record="Define the dynamic dates I'd want and their frequencies">
  Could I have dynamic dates? Instead of specifying an exact date, I specify something like "NextFinancialReview" and the task appears on the appropriate day, with configurable frequencies per dynamic date and the ability to postpone to the next occurrence.
</Question>

<Question
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="moderate"
  why="A date range, rather than a single day, lets a long-running goal show up every day it's active so I don't lose track of it mid-span."
  howOften="When a goal spans a period rather than a single deadline"
  when="When setting up a multi-day or multi-week goal"
  record="Note where range-based goals would help and how they'd display">
  Could I specify a date range for a goal (a start and end date) so the goal applies for the whole period and shows up on every day in that span?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Task dependencies

<SectionBanner why="Tasks rarely stand alone. Many can't start until something else finishes or some event occurs, and modeling that is what keeps me from working things in the wrong order." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  priority="high"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="deep"
  why="If I can't express that task B waits on task A (or on an event), my list lies to me about what's actually workable right now."
  howOften="When planning work with real ordering constraints"
  when="When sequencing a project with prerequisites"
  record="Map the dependency graph: what blocks what, and the order to do them">
  How do I declare that some tasks cannot be started until other events finish? What tasks depend on other tasks? What is blocking my tasks? In what order should tasks be done?
</Question>

<Question
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="deep"
  why="Knowing the exact criterion that makes a task active keeps me from picking up work that isn't ready, which is a quiet waste of effort."
  howOften="When defining when a blocked task becomes workable"
  when="When a task depends on a condition rather than a date"
  record="Write the activation criteria and how dependencies are represented">
  What triggers a task to become active and ready to be worked on? How do I represent dependencies between tasks and events? What is the criteria for beginning work on a task?
</Question>

<Question
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="deep"
  why="Being able to say 'when A completes, start B' or 'when A starts, start B' captures the real shapes of dependency, not just a single naive one."
  howOften="When the dependency is more nuanced than simple completion"
  when="When designing how dependencies are declared"
  record="Note the dependency types I need (on-complete, on-start, on-event) and what else a task can depend on">
  How do I declare task dependencies, including tasks that depend on events occurring and tasks that depend on other tasks (e.g. when task A *completes* start task B, or when task A *starts* start task B)? What else can a task depend on?
</Question>

<Question
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="deep"
  why="Extracting events from my todos and templating their annotations would let a due date appear automatically the moment a condition is met."
  howOften="When building event handling into the tooling"
  when="During deeper design of the task system"
  record="Sketch event extraction and templated annotations like 'beforeEarningX'">
  Could I extract a list of events from these kinds of todos, and template-ize event annotations (like "beforeEarningX")? Could I auto-add a due date as soon as the `@when` condition is met?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Automating tasks

<SectionBanner why="The least valuable work is the bookkeeping of tasks themselves. These questions hunt for what a script could create, recreate, or remind me of without my touch." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power="spark"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Every bit of task creation and reminding I can script is effort I get back. Automation is what keeps the system from becoming its own chore."
  howOften="When looking for friction to remove from the process"
  when="When a piece of task admin keeps repeating by hand"
  record="List what can be automated: creation, recurring management, reminders">
  What task creation can be automated? What repetitive task management can be scripted? How can I automate reminders and recurring tasks?
</Question>

<Question
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="quick"
  why="Tying todo creation to things I already do (stashing commits, photographing a product, adding to a cart) catches intentions at the exact moment they form."
  howOften="When designing automatic capture hooks"
  when="When I notice a real-world action that implies a task"
  record="Note the action-to-todo hooks worth building">
  Should I create todos for the number of stashed commits I have (implying a backlog, since it hasn't been committed)? Should I create a todo for things I want to buy when I take a picture of a product, or when I add an item to my Amazon cart?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Breaking down work

<SectionBanner why="Some work is bigger than a task and deserves to be a story with a reason behind it. These questions are about decomposing effort and knowing why it's worth doing." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Stories carry the 'why' and the shape of a feature; tasks alone can hide the purpose. Knowing when to use which keeps work meaningful, not just numerous."
  howOften="When deciding how to break down a larger piece of work"
  when="At the start of a feature or initiative"
  record="Note the why, the ordered steps, and the resources each task needs">
  What is the advantage of using stories over tasks? Reduced number of tickets? Why (as a company) are we working on this feature? What needs to be done to accomplish this feature, and in what order? Which resources should I allocate to perform a specific task?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Structuring your tasks

<SectionBanner why="Tasks nest: subtasks roll up into tasks, tasks into projects, projects into goals. Seeing that hierarchy is what keeps a flat list from hiding the bigger picture." />

*Note: if weight is at least 1, the task is repetitive: it should happen around once a week.*

The structure of my tasks is as follows:

```
Goal A
     Project A for Goal A
          Research A for Project A
          Task A for Project A
              Subtask A for Task A
         Subtask B for Project A
     Task B for Goal A
     Research B for Goal A
    Subtask C for Goal A
```
