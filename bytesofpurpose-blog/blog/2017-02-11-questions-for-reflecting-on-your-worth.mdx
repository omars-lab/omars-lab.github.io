It's easy to confuse what I've done with what I'm worth. Worth is harder: it's about
the value I create, the skills I actually have, and the way I change how people think
and feel. These are the questions I sit with when I want an honest inventory of myself
rather than a flattering one. I pick the ones that make me pause before I answer.

<!-- truncate -->

## Evaluating yourself

<SectionBanner why="Before I can grow my worth, I have to be able to assess it honestly, without inflating or deflating it. These questions ask for a clear-eyed read on what actually makes me valuable." />

<Question
  power={['fire', 'chisel']}
  priority="core"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="deep"
  why="How I evaluate myself sets the standard I live up or down to. If I measure my worth by the wrong yardstick, I'll chase the wrong proof of it. This question forces me to name the yardstick out loud."
  howOften="Once a year, and any time my sense of self-worth swings hard"
  when="During annual reflection, or after a success or failure that moved how I see myself"
  record="Write the criteria I actually judge myself by, then ask whether those are the right ones">
  How do you evaluate yourself? What makes you valuable?
</Question>

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="high"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="deep"
  why="What I think I'm worth quietly caps what I'll ask for and attempt. Separating my honest self-estimate from what's actually valuable about me exposes where I'm underselling myself or coasting on a flattering story."
  howOften="Annually"
  when="When I'm about to negotiate, ask for something, or decide whether I'm enough for a challenge"
  record="Write my honest number or read, then the list of what's genuinely valuable; compare the two">
  What do you think you're worth? What is valuable about you?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="medium"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Naming my most impressive skills isn't bragging; it's knowing my own toolkit. If I can't list what I'm genuinely good at, I can't deploy it deliberately or build on it."
  howOften="Quarterly, as a capability check"
  when="When deciding what to lead with, or what to develop further"
  record="A ranked short list of my strongest skills, updated as they grow">
  What are your most impressive skills?
</Question>

## How you add value

<SectionBanner why="Worth isn't a static number; it's something I produce by adding value to specific people and places. These questions push me from 'what am I worth' to 'how, where, and to whom do I actually create value.'" />

<Question
  power="anvil"
  priority="high"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="deep"
  why="'How do you add value' is the active version of worth: not what I am, but what I produce for others. Adding the future tense, 'how do you plan on being valuable,' keeps it from being a one-time accounting and makes it a direction."
  howOften="Quarterly, when I plan where to put my effort"
  when="At the start of a season, or when I feel like I'm taking more than I'm giving"
  record="Write how I add value now and the concrete plan for adding more; check the plan against my calendar">
  How do you add value? How do you plan on being valuable?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="high"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="There's a difference between where I could have an impact and where I choose to. The word 'choose' is the point: my impact is finite, so deciding where to aim it is itself a statement of values."
  howOften="Quarterly, when allocating my limited attention"
  when="When I'm spread across too many places to matter in any of them"
  record="List where I genuinely affect things, then pick the few places I'm choosing to focus">
  Where can you have an impact? Where do you choose to affect?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="medium"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Being valuable to myself and being valuable to society are different accounts, and both matter. Checking them together keeps me from neglecting one for the other, since a worth that serves no one and a service that empties me are both incomplete."
  howOften="Annually"
  when="When I notice I'm pouring into others and running dry, or hoarding value and helping no one"
  record="Write my value to myself and to society side by side; look for the imbalance">
  How are you valuable to yourself? To society?
</Question>

## Your impact on others

<SectionBanner why="A real measure of my worth is the change I leave in other people's heads and hearts. These questions ask me to be deliberate about the thoughts and feelings I want to invoke, instead of leaving my effect on people to chance." />

<Question
  power="anvil"
  priority="high"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="deep"
  why="The thoughts I stir in others outlast almost anything else I do. If I'm not clear on what I want them to think, I'm producing influence with no aim. Naming the thoughts gives my work a target."
  howOften="Annually, and before sharing work that will reach people"
  when="When preparing to publish, speak, or lead in any visible way"
  record="Write the thoughts I want someone to leave with, then ask whether my current work produces them">
  What thoughts do you want to invoke from others? How do you want them to structure their thoughts?
</Question>

<Question
  power="spark"
  priority="medium"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Feelings are the part of my impact that people remember longest, often after they've forgotten the details. Deciding what emotion I want to invoke turns my effect on people into something I shape rather than something that just happens."
  howOften="Annually, alongside the thoughts question"
  when="When I want to be intentional about the emotional mark I leave"
  record="Name the feeling I want to create; gather feedback on the feeling I actually leave">
  What emotions do you want to invoke?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="medium"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="How I want to be seen and how I can actually shape that are linked. Asking both keeps the aspiration honest, because wanting to be seen a certain way means nothing unless I'm doing the things that earn it."
  howOften="Annually"
  when="When there's a gap between how I'm perceived and how I want to be"
  record="Write the perception I want, then the concrete behaviors that would actually create it">
  How do you want others to see you? How can you affect how others think, feel, and perceive you?
</Question>

## Skills and talents

<SectionBanner why="My skills are the raw material of my worth, but only if I know them honestly and develop the right ones. These questions take a real inventory of what I can do and decide where to deepen it." />

<Question
  power="spark"
  priority="high"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Knowing my fast ball, the one thing I do better than anything else, tells me what to lead with and lean on. Most people scatter their effort; naming the single best skill lets me build around a real strength."
  howOften="Quarterly, as I sharpen my edge"
  when="When deciding what to specialize in or what to put forward"
  record="Name the one signature skill plainly; if I can't, that's a sign to develop one">
  What is your fast ball, the one thing you do better than anything else?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="high"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="deep"
  why="The gap between the skills I have and the balance I want is a development map. Naming both, and the why behind which to focus on, turns vague self-improvement into a deliberate plan."
  howOften="Quarterly"
  when="When planning what to learn or invest in next"
  record="Two columns, current balance and desired balance; write the why next to each focus choice">
  What talents and abilities do you currently have? Which do you want to focus on, and why? What balance of skills do you have versus the balance you want?
</Question>

<Question
  power="anvil"
  priority="high"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="deep"
  why="A gift left undeveloped is worth withheld from the world and from me. Asking which gift deserves developing, and committing to fully use my talents, is how raw ability turns into actual value instead of unrealized potential."
  howOften="Annually, as a stewardship check"
  when="When I notice a strength I keep neglecting, or feel underused"
  record="Name the gift worth developing and one concrete way I'll fully use it this year">
  What gift of yours is worth developing? What deserves developing? How will you fully utilize your talents?
</Question>

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="medium"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Asking what qualifies me to teach what I know is a tough honesty check on my real competence. If I can't answer it, I may know less than I think; if I can, teaching is one of the highest-value ways to use that knowledge."
  howOften="Annually, or before I set out to teach or mentor"
  when="When I'm tempted to teach something, or doubting whether I've earned the right to"
  record="Write the honest qualification: the experience and depth that back up what I'd teach">
  What qualifies you to teach what you know?
</Question>

## Your contribution

<SectionBanner why="Worth measured at different scales looks different: what I give myself, my family, society, and the planet. These questions widen the lens so my contribution isn't only counted in one narrow place." />

<Question
  power={['anvil', 'fire']}
  priority="core"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="deep"
  why="Measuring my contribution across widening circles, self, family, society, planet, keeps my worth from collapsing into one easy scale. The widest circles are the ones I'm most tempted to ignore, which is exactly why the question matters."
  howOften="Once a year, as a contribution audit"
  when="During year-end reflection, when taking stock of what my life is actually producing"
  record="Write the contribution at each level; the empty levels are where the next work lives">
  What is your contribution to yourself? To your family? To society? To the planet?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="medium"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Pairing 'how' with 'to whom' keeps my plan to be valuable concrete instead of abstract. Value always has a recipient, and naming them stops me from a vague intention to help that never lands anywhere specific."
  howOften="Quarterly, when planning where to direct my value"
  when="When my intention to contribute is real but unfocused"
  record="Write the specific people or groups I'll be valuable to, and how, for each">
  How do you plan on being valuable, and to whom?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="low"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="The resources of the world I draw on, time, attention, materials, opportunity, come with a quiet responsibility. Asking what I'm using reframes my worth as stewardship: am I returning at least as much as I consume?"
  howOften="Annually, as a stewardship reflection"
  when="When taking stock of what I consume versus what I give back"
  record="List the world's resources I rely on, then what I return in exchange">
  What resources of the world are you utilizing?
</Question>

## Taking inventory

<SectionBanner why="Most of my self-assessment is either too flattering or too harsh. These questions ask for a plain, honest inventory: what I actually do well, what I actually know, and what genuinely deserves more of my attention." />

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="high"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="An honest inventory of my skills is the antidote to both impostor feelings and inflated self-belief. Asking whether the inventory is truly honest, not flattering, is what makes it useful rather than comforting."
  howOften="Quarterly, as a deliberate honesty check"
  when="When I suspect I'm either underrating or overrating what I can actually do"
  record="Write the skill list, then stress-test each entry against real evidence I've done it">
  Do you have an honest inventory of the skills you have?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="medium"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="quick"
  why="Splitting 'what do you do well' from 'what do you know' separates skill from knowledge, which I tend to blur. Seeing them apart shows whether I'm strong on doing, on knowing, or lopsided toward one."
  howOften="Quarterly, as a quick inventory"
  when="When I want a fast read on my abilities versus my knowledge"
  record="Two short lists, do-well and know; note where one badly outweighs the other">
  What do you do well? What do you know?
</Question>

<Question
  power="spark"
  priority="medium"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Knowing what deserves more attention to develop turns a static inventory into a growth plan. The word 'deserves' makes me prioritize: not everything I could develop is worth the investment, so this picks the few that are."
  howOften="Quarterly, when choosing what to grow next"
  when="When deciding where to put my limited development energy"
  record="Pick the one or two areas that most deserve deeper investment, and why">
  What deserves more of your attention to develop further?
</Question>
