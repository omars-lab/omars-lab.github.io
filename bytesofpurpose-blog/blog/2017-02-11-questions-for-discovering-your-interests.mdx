Here's the trap with "what are you interested in?" you can't list your interests
on command. You don't magically know them. The honest way in is to *infer* them
from what you already do, read, build, and avoid. These are the questions I use to
reverse-engineer my own curiosity: what draws me, what I'm done with, and what
problems I keep circling back to.

<!-- truncate -->

## Inferring interests from what you do

<SectionBanner why="I can't recite my interests on demand, but my behavior leaks them constantly. These questions read the evidence I'm already producing instead of waiting for a list to appear." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power="spark"
  priority="core"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="What I actually do is more honest than what I say I'm into. Reading my interests off my behavior bypasses the wishful thinking and shows me what genuinely pulls me."
  howOften="Monthly, as a review of how I spent my free attention"
  when="When I want a true read on my interests, not an aspirational one"
  record="List what you actually did this month, then name the interest each action implies">
  How can you infer what you're interested in from what you actually do?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="high"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="A blank-page list of interests reflects who I think I should be. My actions reflect who I am. Mining my behavior closes that gap."
  howOften="Monthly"
  when="When the obvious list of interests feels stale or borrowed"
  record="Track recurring behaviors and let the pattern name the interest">
  You can't list your interests on command, so what do your actions and behaviors reveal?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="medium"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Interests hide in the things I do so automatically I forget they're choices. Deliberately scanning for the overlooked ones keeps my self-picture from going stale."
  howOften="Quarterly, when taking stock"
  when="When I suspect I've boxed myself into a too-narrow set of interests"
  record="Note the interests you almost forgot to count, the quiet ones">
  How do you make sure you're not overlooking an interest when you take stock?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Problems and questions

<Question
  power="fire"

  record="List the problems you'd work on even if no one asked you to">

<Question

  howOften="Quarterly"

<Question

## Identifying your passions

<SectionBanner why="Passions are interests that have hardened into something I'll return to without prompting. These questions help me tell a real passion apart from a passing enthusiasm." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="core"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="deep"
  why="A passion is what survives boredom, difficulty, and a lack of applause. Pinning down what makes something a passion (not just a hobby) tells me where to point a life, not just an afternoon."
  howOften="Once a year, as a deep reflection"
  when="When deciding what to organize my life around"
  record="Write your definition of a passion, then test your candidates against it">
  How can you pinpoint your different passions? What makes a passion a passion?
</Question>

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="high"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="deep"
  why="'What do you love?' is easy to answer wishfully, so I need a way to be sure. Loving the practice and not just the idea is the test that separates a passion from a fantasy."
  howOften="Yearly"
  when="When I'm tempted to call something a passion prematurely"
  record="Write what you love, then look for evidence you keep returning to it">
  What do you love? How can you be sure you've found a passion?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="medium"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="My actions are the most reliable witness to my passions. Reflecting on what I repeatedly chose to do reveals passions I might never name on a blank page."
  howOften="Yearly"
  when="When my stated passions and my behavior seem to disagree"
  record="Trace your recurring actions back to the passion driving them">
  Can you determine your passions by reflecting on your actions?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="medium"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="The books I read, the things I want to study, and the projects I finish form a portfolio of evidence. Reading my passion off that portfolio is more honest than guessing."
  howOften="Yearly"
  when="When I want proof, not a hunch, about a passion"
  record="Inventory your books, studies, and finished projects and look for the through-line">
  Is your passion captured in the books you read, the things you want to study, the
  projects you've completed?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Problems worth dedicating yourself to

<Question
  power={['fire', 'anvil']}
  priority="core"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="deep"

  howOften="Yearly, as a long-horizon question"
  when="During deep reflection on direction"
  record="Name the one problem you'd give years to, and why it stays interesting">

  record="List the challenges you'd accept for yourself and for others">

<Question

  howOften="Yearly"
  when="When defining the point of a project, not just its mechanics"

<Question
