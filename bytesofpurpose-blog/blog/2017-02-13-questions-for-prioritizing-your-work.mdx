Capturing every idea is the easy part. The hard part is deciding what to do *now* from a pile of initiatives, projects, and things I want to learn. These are the questions I ask myself to focus, weigh trade-offs, deprioritize what no longer matters, and lay it all out on a roadmap. Read them slowly and answer the ones that snag on something.

<!-- truncate -->

## Current focus

<SectionBanner why="Everything starts with 'now.' Before any framework, I name the single thing I most need to be working on at this moment." />

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="high"
  cron="daily"
  depth="quick"
  why="From a pile of possible work, only one thing can be the most valuable right now. Naming it cuts through the paralysis of too many options."
  howOften="Daily, when choosing what to work on"
  when="At the start of a work session"
  record="Name the single most valuable thing I can do now, and what I genuinely need to be doing">
  What question(s) am I focused on answering now? What initiatives have I decided to work on now? What projects should I work on, and what things should I learn now? What's the most valuable thing I can do now? What do I **need** to be working on right now?
</Question>

## How I prioritize

<SectionBanner why="Naming the now is easy; defending it is the work. This is the method underneath the choice: how I actually decide what's important and what wins." />

<Question
  power="anvil"
  priority="high"
  cron="weekly"
  depth="deep"
  why="If I can't articulate how I decide what's important, my priorities are really just whatever feels urgent or appealing in the moment."
  howOften="Weekly, when setting the week's focus"
  when="When choosing one initiative over another"
  record="Write the basis I'm deciding on and which goals each candidate serves">
  How do I prioritize my initiatives? How do I determine what I should and shouldn't do? How do I decide what is important? On what basis do I decide to work on one thing over another? What goals do each of the priorities at hand help me attain?
</Question>

<Question
  power="spark"
  cron="weekly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Desire quietly tilts what I call 'important.' Naming that pull keeps me honest about when I'm choosing on merit versus on mood."
  howOften="Weekly, and whenever a choice feels driven by impulse"
  when="When I notice I'm leaning toward what I feel like doing"
  record="Note where my desires are steering my goals, and whether that's the right call">
  How are my desires influencing the goals I focus on? What do I feel like doing right now? When do I actually decide to work on something?
</Question>

<Question
  cron="weekly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Sorting what I need to learn from what I merely want to learn, and seeing each candidate's dependencies, turns a flat list into a ranked one."
  howOften="Weekly, when ordering learning objectives"
  when="When my learning backlog gets too long to act on"
  record="Sort objectives by need-vs-want and note what each depends on">
  What do my candidates depend on, and what are the ultimate goal(s) I'm prioritizing against? How will I sort my learning objectives/initiatives? Which do I NEED to learn vs WANT to learn? Which ones have I recently started?
</Question>

## Prioritization factors

<SectionBanner why="A good priority decision rests on factors I can name and weigh. These questions make those factors explicit so the call isn't pure gut feel." />

<Question
  power="spark"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="deep"
  why="If I can't name the factors I weigh, or which factor outranks which, I can't make a consistent decision twice. Explicit factors make priority repeatable."
  howOften="When refining how I make priority calls"
  when="When two candidates feel equally worthy"
  record="List my prioritization factors and rank them against each other">
  What factors should I consider when prioritizing? What must I consider? How do the factors compare, and which factor is more important than the others? How do I determine this?
</Question>

<Question
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Ease, urgency, and interest are quick, practical tiebreakers when the deeper factors come out even. They keep me moving instead of stalling on analysis."
  howOften="When breaking a tie between candidates"
  when="When several options pass the main filters"
  record="Note which is easiest, which is most urgent, and which I'm most drawn to">
  Which one is easier to do? Which one needs to be done now? Which ones am I most interested in doing? How often should I address each of these concerns? What's the difference between a factor and a concern?
</Question>

Principles:

- A task is higher in priority if it overlaps with many of my goals.
- A task is higher in priority if it needs to be done immediately and is important.

## By life category

<SectionBanner why="Priorities aren't only about work. Sweeping across career, growth, organization, learning, and family makes sure no whole area of life gets silently dropped." />

<Question
  priority="high"
  cron="weekly"
  depth="moderate"

## Balancing priorities

<SectionBanner why="Two things constantly compete for my time: doing and learning. This question is about holding that balance instead of letting one starve the other." />

<Question
  cron="weekly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="If I only ever do, I stop growing; if I only ever learn, I stop shipping. Naming the balance keeps both in play."
  howOften="Weekly, when allocating time"
  when="When learning and doing are competing for the same hours"
  record="Note the split between learning and projects, and what I must learn before I can do new things">

## What gets prioritized

<SectionBanner why="Before prioritizing, it helps to know what's even in the running and where it lives. This question is about taking inventory across roles and tools." />

<Question
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="I can't prioritize what I can't see. If candidates are scattered across Jira, roles, and a roadmap, taking inventory is the prerequisite to any ranking."
  howOften="Monthly, as an inventory sweep"
  when="When my candidates feel scattered or incomplete"
  record="List what I'm prioritizing and where each item lives; decide what goes on the roadmap vs the task manager">
  What goes on the roadmap, and what goes straight to my task manager? What do I prioritize, and what don't I prioritize? What are the things I am prioritizing, and where are they found? Are these in Jira? Are they scattered across roles? What initiatives am I prioritizing?
</Question>

## Prioritizing projects

<SectionBanner why="Projects are the big bets, so they deserve their own filter. These questions check a project against what I'll learn, enjoy, and can share before I commit." />

<Question
  power="spark"
  priority="high"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="A project is a large commitment of time, so it should clear a bar: does it teach me something, overlap with what I enjoy, and is it worth doing first?"
  howOften="Monthly, or when choosing the next project"
  when="When deciding which project to start"
  record="Score each project against learning, enjoyment, marketability, and distributability">
  How do I figure out what project(s) I should work on? How many of these does it check off: will it help me learn what I want to learn, does it overlap with what I enjoy, do I want to work on it? How marketable is this project? How distributable is it? What else should I consider? What projects am I passionate about? Of all the things I want to do, what should I do first?
</Question>

I generally work on projects for one of two reasons: to learn something new, or to save myself time.

### Aligning projects to objectives

<SectionBanner why="A project earns its place by advancing a real objective. These questions force each project to declare which goal it serves and what it'll teach me." />

<Question
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="A project that doesn't advance an objective is a hobby wearing a work costume. Mapping each one to a goal keeps my effort pointed somewhere."
  howOften="Monthly, when reviewing the project slate"
  when="When justifying why a project is on the list"
  record="For each project, name the objective it advances, the result, and what it'll teach me">
  What projects help me advance specific objectives, and what objectives does each project help me meet? Why should I work on specific projects? What is the result of working on each, and what will each teach me? What projects can advance my self-marketing objective (are any in a repo I can share, will any help me learn what I want to learn)?
</Question>

### Projects reflecting priorities

<SectionBanner why="My projects should be a mirror of my priorities. When they aren't, that mismatch is the signal that I'm working on the wrong things." />

<Question
  power="fire"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="deep"
  why="If my actual projects don't reflect my stated priorities, one of them is lying. Catching that gap is how I realign action with intent."
  howOften="Monthly, as an alignment check"
  when="When I suspect my work has drifted from my priorities"
  record="Note whether my projects reflect my priorities, and where they diverge from what I want to learn">
  How do my priorities affect the projects I actually work on? Do my projects reflect my priorities? Am I working on projects that push me to explore the things I want to do and learn? How do the things I work on align with what I want to learn? Can I commit to a personal project where I apply the things I learn?
</Question>

### Evaluating projects

<SectionBanner why="Once a project is in motion, it still needs a rubric. These questions are how I judge each project's benefit, alignment, and what it showcases about me." />

<Question
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="A consistent evaluation rubric (benefit, interest-alignment, learning overlap, what it showcases) keeps me from overrating projects just because they're already underway."
  howOften="Monthly, when reviewing active projects"
  when="When deciding whether a project still deserves my time"
  record="Score each project on benefit, alignment, learning overlap, and the skills it showcases">
  How will I evaluate each project? Why do I want to work on each, and what are the benefits? Which do I want to work on the most? How much does each align with my interests, overlap with what I want to learn, and reflect how I market myself? What does each project showcase about my skills, technical capabilities, and product design capabilities?
</Question>

## Deprioritizing

<SectionBanner why="Prioritizing is also about subtraction. These questions are the hard ones: what do I stop, drop, or admit I no longer actually want?" />

<Question
  power="chisel"
  priority="high"
  cron="weekly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="A list only stays meaningful if things leave it. Naming what to stop is what makes room for what matters; deprioritizing is half of prioritizing."
  howOften="Weekly, as a pruning pass"
  when="When my list has grown faster than I can act on it"
  record="List what to stop, what to deprioritize, and the wants I've outgrown">
  What should I stop doing? What should I deprioritize? What am I spending time on that isn't important? What are the things I wanted to do at some point that I no longer want to do?
</Question>

## Constructing a roadmap

<SectionBanner why="Priorities laid on a timeline become a roadmap. These questions place my goals in time and short-vs-long-term order so I can see the whole arc at once." />

<Question
  power={['fire', 'anvil']}
  priority="core"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="deep"
  why="A roadmap turns a flat list of goals into a sequence across time. Seeing where goals intersect, and which must come first, is what makes a long-term vision achievable."
  howOften="Quarterly, when laying out the bigger picture"
  when="When planning across months and years"
  record="Plot goals on a timeline, classified short vs long term, with their intersections and ordering">
  Can I see my goals over time, on a timeline? Can I classify my goals into short-term and long-term, and see timelines for each type? What are my desired end-goals by end of year and next year? Where do I see myself in 30 years, 20 years, 10 years? What is my vision for myself, and where do I want my goals to take me? How and where do all of my goals and milestones intersect? What goals are more important to complete than others? Given dependencies between goals, which should be prioritized first to attain them all in a timely fashion? Which activities should I focus on to collectively attain my goals?
</Question>

## Tracking dependencies

<SectionBanner why="Goals lean on other goals. Knowing which depend on which, and what's in the way, keeps me from chasing a goal whose prerequisites aren't ready." />

<Question
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="A goal blocked by an unmet dependency isn't workable yet, no matter how much I want it. Mapping that keeps me from pushing on what can't move."
  howOften="Monthly, when reviewing goal progress"
  when="When a goal feels stuck or out of reach"
  record="Note which goals depend on others, and the obstacles in the way of each">
  What goals depend on other goals/activities? What obstacles are hindering me from accomplishing my goals?
</Question>

## Retrospecting

<SectionBanner why="Prioritization is a skill, and skills improve by review. These questions close the loop: how well did my last set of choices actually serve me?" />

<Question
  power="chisel"
  priority="high"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="deep"
  why="The only way to prioritize better is to grade how I prioritized before. Naming what I shouldn't have done, and what I missed, is how the skill compounds."
  howOften="Monthly, as a retrospective"
  when="At the end of a month or a project cycle"
  record="Write what worked, what I wrongly prioritized, what I missed, and how to get better">
  How well did my prioritization work? What did I prioritize that I shouldn't have? What did I miss? How can I get better at prioritizing? What shortcomings have I observed?
</Question>
