Priorities only become real when they meet a calendar. Planning is how you translate "what matters" into time-bound action across horizons: today, this week, this quarter, this year, and the whole arc of a life. Use the questions below as prompts. Pick the horizon you're working in, and answer honestly rather than aspirationally.

<!-- truncate -->

## Understanding time and planning

- This is NOT *what should I spend my time on*, but rather *what do I actually spend my time on*.
- How am I spending my time?
- What should be planned?
- How are we planning for the future?
- What makes a good plan? What questions should a plan address?
- How often should I plan?
- Is prioritizing a part of planning? Or do I figure out my priorities first, and then figure out how to collectively realize them?
- As a do-er, I grow a list of things I want to do. As a planner, I figure out what I should do first.
- What is planning? What does it mean to plan?

A few framing ideas worth holding onto:

- Planning is part of time management; it is preparing for a future.
- A plan is a sequential set of steps to help everyone reach a common goal.
- If we don't all adhere to the same plan, we may step on each other's toes.
- A plan considers priorities and chains them together.

*Further reading:* [Time management vs. task management](https://toggl.com/blog/time-management-vs-task-management) and [Planning in time management](https://www.managementstudyguide.com/planning-in-time-management.htm).

## Knowing why you're doing what you're doing

- I need to always know why I am doing what I am doing, and be able to justify why I am not doing something else.
- At a given point in time, I need to know what I am working on.
- As a programmer, what features am I working on?
- As a learner, what am I learning?
- As a doer, what projects am I working on?

I don't know when I'll be asked for a status update, but I need to be confident and clear, because I should always know what I am doing and why I am doing it.

## Recurring activities

- What are the things I constantly find myself doing? Which of these am I doing?
- Learning something new?
- Doing something interesting?
- Organizing myself?
- Fixing a gap or weakness?
- Marketing myself?

## Daily planning

- What should I accomplish today?
- What is my plan for today?

## Weekly planning

- What should we refine from last week?
- What should we introduce this week?
- What are we planning on doing next week?
- How should we align what we do this week to make next week easier to work with?

## Quarterly planning

- What should I accomplish this quarter?
- What are my quarterly objectives?
- How does this quarter contribute to my yearly goals?
- Have I snapshotted my learning plans so I know what I've committed myself to learn over time?

## Yearly planning

- What must I accomplish this year?
- What will I do differently this year?
- What books will I read this year?
- What scripts will I write this year?
- What topics will I learn this year?
- This year, it will be valuable if I…

## Lifetime timeline

- Where do I see myself in 30 years, 20 years, 10 years, 5 years, 1 year?
- What is the trajectory of my life?
- What major milestones do I want to hit?

## Planning techniques

Determining planning frequencies:

- How often do I "replan"?
- Weekly: put together weekly plans and kick them off on the weekend.
- Keep a Venn diagram each week: how much new stuff do I want to do this week, versus how much carries over from last week?
- Publish the things I do on a weekly basis.
- Quarterly: plan for work and learnings.

Creating learning plans:

- How much time should be considered in each chunk of the plan?
- A learning plan should be in 10-week chunks: what can I learn in 10 weeks?
- How much time will I spend each day?

## Reflecting on and assessing plans

- What functionality is valuable?
- What is my function to society?
- What am I passionate about producing? What am I passionate about consuming?
- Am I on track with my plan? What adjustments need to be made?
- How do I reflect on my plans?
- How often are my learning checkpoints? How often do I re-evaluate my learnings?
- How often do I assess my trajectory?
- When can I assess how long it will take to reach the end?
- How often do I generate progress reports along the way?
- How often do I re-prioritize?
- When do I decide whether to continue down this path, or switch to something more beneficial for the time?
- Are the things I am pursuing the best things to learn right now, considering all the things I could be doing?
