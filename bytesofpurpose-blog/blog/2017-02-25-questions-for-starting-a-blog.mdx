Before you spin up a blog, it helps to settle a handful of questions first. Here is a five-step set to work through, from why you're doing it to how you'll get it in front of people. Answer these and the rest gets a lot easier.

<!-- truncate -->

## Step 1: Why?

<SectionBanner why="Every later decision (the name, the cadence, the tech, the marketing) inherits its answer from this one. A blog without a clear why drifts and dies, so it is worth getting honest about purpose before writing a single post." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power="spark"
  priority="core"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="The reason you start the blog sets its direction, its tone, and how long you will stick with it. If you cannot say what you will gain or what vision you are chasing, motivation evaporates the first time it gets hard."
  howOften="Before you start, and revisit quarterly to check the blog still serves the why"
  when="At the very beginning, and whenever you feel like quitting"
  record="Write a short statement of purpose and vision; revisit it when momentum dips">
  Why do I want to start a blog? What purpose will it serve? What will I gain out of it? What is my vision for it? How long do I plan on maintaining it?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Step 2: The name

<SectionBanner why="The name is the first impression and the hardest thing to change later. A good one captures the vision from Step 1 in a few words, so it is worth pausing on rather than grabbing the first available domain." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power="chisel"
  priority="high"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="moderate"
  why="A name that captures your vision does a lot of marketing work for free. Picking it deliberately, anchored to the purpose you just defined, beats settling for whatever was available."
  howOften="Once, up front, with a deliberate brainstorm"
  when="After you have a clear vision and are ready to commit to an identity"
  record="List candidate names and check each against your vision; pick the one that fits best">
  Now that you have a vision, what name captures it?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Step 3: What content, and how often?

<SectionBanner why="Consistency is what separates a living blog from an abandoned one. Deciding the cadence up front (and what triggers a new post) turns writing from a someday intention into a commitment you can actually keep." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  priority="high"
  cron="weekly"
  depth="quick"
  why="A blog only compounds if you keep feeding it. Committing to a trigger (a filled notebook, an idea worth sharing, or a set time frame elapsing) removes the daily debate about whether to write and replaces it with a rule."
  howOften="Decide once, then enforce it on your chosen cadence"
  when="When setting up your writing routine, and whenever the cadence slips"
  record="Write down your posting trigger and cadence, and track whether you are hitting it">
  Will you be consistently writing posts for the blog? If so, how often? Commit yourself to creating a new post every so often: every time your notebook fills up, every time you have an idea worth sharing, or every time some chosen time frame goes by without a post.
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Step 4: What technology?

<SectionBanner why="The tech stack decides how much you will fight your tools instead of writing. These choices (hosted versus self-run, your own UI and backend, what you embed) trade convenience against control, and the right answer depends entirely on your why." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  priority="medium"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Hosting and platform choices set your ceiling and your maintenance burden. A public host is fast to start; your own server, cloud setup, UI, or backend buys control at the cost of effort. Knowing what you want to embed shapes which path is even viable."
  howOften="Once, during setup, and again only if you outgrow your choice"
  when="When standing up the blog, and when you hit a limitation of your current platform"
  record="List your requirements (including anything you want to embed) and match a platform to them">
  Will you be using a public blog hosting site? Will you run your own server? Will you run your blog on the cloud? Will you create your own UI for the blog? Will you create your own backend for the blog? What do you plan on embedding into your blog?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Step 5: How will you market it?

<SectionBanner why="Writing into the void is discouraging; even a little distribution changes everything. Deciding how people will find your blog (your resume, your friends, catchy titles, answering questions people are already asking) turns a private journal into something that reaches readers." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="high"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Great posts that nobody sees do not build anything. Planning your channels up front (putting it on your resume, telling friends, writing catchy titles, finding questions people ask and referencing your posts in answers) gives the writing a chance to land."
  howOften="Monthly, as you build distribution habits"
  when="After you have a few posts worth sharing, and when growth stalls"
  record="Pick the channels you will actually use and track which ones bring readers">
  How will you make others aware of your blog? Will you put it on your resume? Will you tell your friends about it? Will you use catchy titles? Will you find questions people are asking and reference it in answers?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>
