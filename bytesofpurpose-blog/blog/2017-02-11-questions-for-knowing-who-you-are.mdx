Why do others often know more about me than I know about myself? I rarely sit down
and study the one subject I have the most data on. These are the questions I return
to when I want to examine who I actually am: my character, my values, the beliefs and
emotions quietly steering me, and the story I keep feeding myself.

<!-- truncate -->

## Defining who you are

<SectionBanner why="If I can't name who I am in plain terms, every other answer rests on sand. Getting a working definition of myself is the foundation everything else builds on." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power={['fire', 'anvil']}
  priority="core"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="deep"
  why="This is the question underneath all the others. If I can't say who I am, I'll keep borrowing an identity from whoever is in the room. The 'why should anyone care' part keeps the answer honest instead of flattering."
  howOften="Once a year, and any time I notice I'm performing a version of myself"
  when="During quiet reflection, journaling, or after a season that changed me"
  record="Write a paragraph and date it; compare year over year to see who I'm becoming">
  Who are you? Who should you be? Why should anyone care?
</Question>

<Question
  power="chisel"
  priority="high"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="deep"
  why="A definition forces me to compress myself into something I can actually hold. 'The gist of you' and 'your reality' push past the resume and toward the thing that's actually true."
  howOften="Annually, alongside the question above"
  when="When I'm tempted to describe myself by titles and roles instead of substance"
  record="Write one or two sentences. If I need a paragraph, I haven't found the gist yet">
  How do you define yourself? What is the gist of you? What is your reality?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Examining your character

<SectionBanner why="Character is what's left when the performance stops, and the story I tell myself about myself quietly becomes that character. Watching the inner dialog is how I stay the author of it instead of the product of it." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power="chisel"
  priority="high"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Naming the vital elements of my character tells me what to protect and what to grow. Without that list, I improve at random instead of on purpose."
  howOften="Quarterly: character moves slowly, but the check keeps it in view"
  when="When reviewing how I actually behaved this season, not how I meant to"
  record="List the core traits, then mark which ones I lived up to and which I didn't">
  What are the vital elements of your character?
</Question>

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="medium"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="The story I feed myself becomes the script I act out. If it's a story of limitation or resentment, I'll live it without noticing. Catching the story is how I get to rewrite it."
  howOften="Monthly, and any time I notice a familiar negative loop"
  when="When I catch myself repeating the same explanation for why things are the way they are"
  record="Write down the story in one line, then write the truer version next to it">
  What story do you feed yourself?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

Pay attention to your inner dialog. It's writing you whether you watch it or not.

## Values and beliefs

<SectionBanner why="My values and beliefs are the code my decisions quietly run on. Making them explicit lets me update them on purpose instead of being governed by ones I inherited by accident." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power="chisel"
  priority="core"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="deep"
  why="Values and beliefs are the operating system underneath every choice I make. Most people never read their own code; they just execute it. Writing it out lets me see what I'm actually running on."
  howOften="Once a year. Values shift slowly, but they do shift"
  when="During annual reflection, or when I notice a gap between what I claim to believe and how I act"
  record="Write them as a list; compare across years to see what deepened and what faded">
  What are your values? What are your beliefs?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="medium"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="quick"
  why="What's important to me right now is a moving target, and naming it stops me from defaulting to whatever is loudest. It's a quick gut-check that keeps my attention pointed where I actually want it."
  howOften="Monthly: priorities drift faster than values do"
  when="At the start of a month, or when I feel scattered across too many things"
  record="A short list of what genuinely matters this month, in order">
  What is currently important to you?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="low"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="The 100-year lens separates the beliefs that are really mine from the ones that are just reactions to my moment. What survives that filter is closer to a conviction than an opinion."
  howOften="Once a year, as a perspective reset"
  when="When I'm worked up over something that feels urgent but may not be lasting"
  record="Write which beliefs I'd still stand behind in a century and which I'd let go">
  Will your thoughts and beliefs matter in 100 years?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## What controls and shapes you

<SectionBanner why="I'm being steered by needs, fears, and constraints I didn't consciously choose, and some of them I've gotten too comfortable inside. Naming what controls me is the first step to deciding what I'll actually allow." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="high"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="deep"
  why="The needs and emotions I haven't named are the ones running me. Naming what controls me is how I take back the wheel. The uncomfortable answers here are usually the truest."
  howOften="Quarterly, and any time I react in a way that surprises me"
  when="After an outsized emotional reaction, or when I feel driven by something I can't name"
  record="Write the honest list, even the parts I'd rather not admit are steering me">
  What needs, beliefs, and emotions are controlling you?
</Question>

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="high"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Some of my limits are real and some I built myself, and I can't tell them apart until I separate the two. The ones others imposed I can negotiate; the ones I imposed I can simply choose to drop."
  howOften="Quarterly, when I feel boxed in"
  when="When I catch myself saying 'I can't' before checking whether that's actually true"
  record="Two columns: constraints I put on myself, constraints others put on me. Then mark which are real">
  What constraints have you imposed on yourself? What constraints have others imposed on you?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="medium"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="I can't control what I feel, but I can decide what I'll let affect me. The word 'let' is the whole point: it moves me from passenger to driver of my own influences."
  howOften="Monthly, as a check on what I'm absorbing"
  when="When I notice I've taken on someone else's mood, opinion, or fear"
  record="List the influences I'll keep, and the ones I'm choosing to stop feeding">
  Which influences, motivators, de-motivators, and emotions will you *let* affect you?
</Question>

<Question
  power={['fire', 'chisel']}
  priority="high"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="deep"
  why="Comfort in the darkness is the most dangerous kind, because it doesn't feel like a problem. This question asks why I've made peace with a place I shouldn't stay, which is usually the first honest step out of it."
  howOften="Quarterly, and whenever I notice I've stopped trying to leave a bad place"
  when="When something painful has started to feel normal and I've stopped fighting it"
  record="Write what the darkness gives me that I'm afraid to give up; that's what keeps me there">
  Why have you gotten comfortable in the darkness?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Becoming self-aware

<SectionBanner why="Self-awareness is the one study I keep postponing on the subject I know least. These questions push me to start the work and to define what 'aware' would even look like, so it's a destination and not a vague intention." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power="spark"
  priority="high"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="quick"
  why="I keep treating self-study as something I'll get to later. This question asks why later never comes. The honest answer is usually that I'm a little afraid of what I'll find."
  howOften="Whenever I notice I've been avoiding looking inward"
  when="When I realize I've analyzed everyone and everything except myself"
  record="A one-line honest answer about what I'm avoiding, then a date to actually start">
  When will you start analyzing yourself? Why don't you study yourself?
</Question>

<Question
  power="spark"
  priority="medium"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="It's strange that the people around me can read me better than I read myself. Sitting with that gap is humbling, and it points directly at the blind spots I most need to close."
  howOften="Once a year, as a humility check"
  when="When someone close to me names a pattern in me I hadn't seen"
  record="Note what others see that I miss; that list is a map of my blind spots">
  Why do others know more about you than you know about yourself?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="medium"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="deep"
  why="If I can't say what 'self-aware' would look like, I can't tell whether I'm getting there. Defining the destination and its stages turns a vague aspiration into something I can actually pursue."
  howOften="Annually, as I refine what the goal even is"
  when="During deeper reflection, when I want a way to measure inner progress"
  record="Write a working definition of self-awareness, then the stages I think it moves through">
  How do you become self-aware? What must you do to declare yourself self-aware? What is your process for reaching a full state of self-awareness, and what are its different states?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Capturing the essentials

<SectionBanner why="Sometimes I need a fast inventory of myself instead of a deep dive: what I think about, what I'm good at, what stops me, and how I get past it. This compact battery takes that snapshot quickly." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  priority="medium"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="quick"
  why="What I keep thinking about is data about who I am, and most of it slips by unexamined. Catching my recurring thoughts, interests, and strengths in one pass gives me a quick read on where my mind actually lives."
  howOften="Monthly, as a quick self-inventory"
  when="When I want a fast snapshot rather than a long reflection session"
  record="A short list under each prompt; the patterns across months matter more than any single answer">
  What do you always think about? What interests you? What are your strengths?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="high"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Purpose, goals, and dreams are the directional questions, and answering them together shows whether they actually point the same way. When they don't line up, that misalignment is the thing to fix."
  howOften="Quarterly, when I reset my direction"
  when="At the start of a season, or when I feel like I'm working hard without aim"
  record="Write all three; check whether the goals and dreams actually serve the stated purpose">
  What is your purpose? What are your goals? What are your dreams?
</Question>

<Question
  power="spark"
  priority="medium"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Knowing my obstacles and what pushes me past them turns being stuck into a solvable problem. The trick of overcoming thought X by remembering thought Y is a practical tool I can actually use in the moment."
  howOften="Monthly, and any time I hit a wall"
  when="When I'm blocked and want to remember what has gotten me unstuck before"
  record="Pair each obstacle with the specific thought or move that breaks it">
  What are your obstacles? What motivates you? What thoughts push you past your obstacles? You can overcome thought X by remembering thought Y. How can you manage your emotions?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Learning about yourself through what's around you

<SectionBanner why="I'm not the only mirror I have. Watching society, nature, and the divine reflects parts of me back that I can't see from the inside, and that outside view often teaches me more about myself than introspection alone." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  priority="low"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="moderate"
  why="The tendencies I notice in society are often my own, magnified. Studying the crowd is a sideways way to study myself, since what I judge or admire in others usually lives in me too."
  howOften="Now and then, when I catch a strong reaction to how people behave"
  when="When something in society repels or attracts me strongly"
  record="Note what I react to in others, then ask honestly where that same thing lives in me">
  How can you learn more about yourself by studying the natural tendencies of society?
</Question>

<Question
  power="spark"
  priority="medium"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="deep"
  why="Looking at myself through the lens of God, and through the 99 names, reframes self-study as something larger than self-improvement. Asking whether I reflect even a little of those attributes turns abstract faith into a concrete mirror."
  howOften="During seasons of faith and searching, not on a fixed schedule"
  when="During prayer, spiritual reflection, or when I want a higher reference point than myself"
  record="Note which attributes I see faint traces of in myself and which I'm furthest from">
  How can you learn more about yourself from God? How does that push you to become more self-aware? Do you minimally reflect any of the 99 names of God?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## "I am" statements

<SectionBanner why="The way I finish 'I am' and 'I will' quietly declares my identity, and I can be guessed by it. Checking whether those statements line up with what I claim to be passionate about is how I tell who I really am from who I say I am." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power="anvil"
  priority="core"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="deep"
  why="My 'I am' statements are the most compressed version of my identity I have. 'You are more than what you have become' is the reminder that I get to declare who I am rather than settle for who I've drifted into."
  howOften="Once a year, written deliberately"
  when="During annual reflection, or when I feel like I've shrunk into a lesser version of myself"
  record="Write the full list of 'I am' statements and read them back as a declaration, not a description">
  What are your "I am" statements? "Remember who you are. You are more than what you have become."
</Question>

<Question
  power="chisel"
  priority="high"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="If someone could guess me from my 'I am's' and 'I will's', then those statements are doing the work of defining me. Checking whether they align with what I say I'm passionate about reveals where my words and my real self disagree."
  howOften="Annually, alongside writing the statements"
  when="When I want to test whether my declared identity and my declared passion actually match"
  record="Line up the 'I am's' against the passion; circle every place they contradict">
  Can you guess who you are based on your "I am's" and your "I will's"? Do your "I am" statements align with what you believe your passion is?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Manifesting your beliefs

<SectionBanner why="Beliefs that stay inside my head don't shape anything. These questions push me to encode what I believe into something tangible, because the act of making values real is where identity stops being a feeling and starts being a contribution." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power={['fire', 'anvil']}
  priority="core"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="deep"
  why="What I truly believe in is the engine under everything I attempt. The conviction that I have something special to give the world isn't arrogance; it's the belief that has to come first, before I'll do the hard work of giving it."
  howOften="Once a year, and whenever doubt starts eroding the belief"
  when="Before committing to something hard, or when I'm tempted to play smaller than I should"
  record="Write the belief as a flat statement I actually act on, not an aspiration I admire">
  What do you truly believe in? I believe I have something special to give this world.
</Question>

<Question
  power="anvil"
  priority="high"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="deep"
  why="A belief I never encode stays a private feeling. Turning values into something tangible, a habit, a creation, a commitment, is how belief becomes evidence. This question asks for the concrete 'how,' not just the conviction."
  howOften="Quarterly, when I check whether my beliefs have produced anything"
  when="When I notice I believe a lot but have little to show for it in the real world"
  record="For each core belief, name one tangible thing it has produced or will produce">
  We have an opportunity to encode our beliefs, to make our values tangible. How will you do that?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>
