This is an early, evolving catalog of the questions I find worth answering. It is more raw and aphoristic than my later question sets, and that is the point: it is a place to gather what I am curious about, figure out where I need to understand more, and consolidate the questions worth pursuing as part of a larger quest. Treat it as a living list, not a finished argument.

<!-- truncate -->

## Value

Where are people more likely to spend their time and money?

## Avoiding Failure

What makes people fail?

- Lack of a certain resource

## Measurement

- What will I measure?
- What questions will I always ask myself?

- What resources do I have available?
- What is special about each of my resources?

### Opportunity

- Where is the opportunity?
- Where is there a knowledge arbitrage?
- Where is there time arbitrage?

### Purpose

- What do I want to do?
- What "idea" is infatuating you?

### Motivation

- Why have you decided to organize yourself?
- Why have you decided to do what you are doing?

### Metrics

- How many questions am I answering per book?

## Learnings

- What questions do I enjoy answering?

## Spending

- What do people spend most of their money on? Their time?
- Why do people spend their money? Their time?
- Can you infer personal attributes about someone from a home they are interested in, based on the different features of the house?
- Where are people spending their time?

## Data

- Where is the data at? The SEC?

## Resources

- What resources do companies possess?

## Planning

- How do you put together a plan for yourself?
- How do you put a plan together for an entire country?
- How can you extract someone's plan from history?
- Who mastered planning?
- Is planning needed, or direction?
- What level of planning is needed to be successful?
- Are you being too granular?

## Thinking

Who is after automating thought?

- Automating thinking?
- Automating creativity?
- Automating ideation?
- Automating all of the ideas? Is it a good idea to do so? Why do we want to do so?

## Growing

- What happens if people stop growing?
- What happens if companies stop growing?

## Purpose

- Do I want to be creating products?
- How does one create products?
- What are some examples of good products?

**I like products because they interface with people's carnal desires?**

## Society

I want to better understand people: detecting the problems people have, and knowing how they respond to those problems. I want to lay the foundation for helping people better understand people.

- How can technology help society?
- How can technology help understand society?
- Can we sift through social media to see where the average person complains the most?
- Can we see the most prominent problems in different parts of the world?

**Who has spent the time to model people's interests?**

## Questions on Questions

- How do I organize my questions?
- Can questions inherit from each other? On what basis?
- How do I map questions to their essential contexts?
- What contexts am I tracking?
- Are the dependencies of the contexts the same as the dependencies of the questions?
- Do your questions paint a complete picture?
- Are you asking the same thing twice?
- What questions are you ready to answer?
- What do you need to do to be able to come up with an answer?
- What questions should you always ask yourself?
- What are the questions you need to ask yourself?

## Inference

- How do you do collective inference?
- On what basis is a possible inference amplified or reduced?
- How accurate is our inference?
- Where do you want your word to be trusted?
- Where do you want to be credible?
- Into what will you put your time?
- What kinds of questions do you want to answer for the rest of your life?
- What will we do for evidence?
- Am I qualified to answer this question?
- What must I know to answer this question?
