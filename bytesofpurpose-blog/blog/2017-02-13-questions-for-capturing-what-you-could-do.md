Before you can decide what to work on now, you have to see the whole field of what you *could* do. Prioritizing too early quietly throws away options you never even named. These questions are for the capture step: brainstorming and organizing your goals, commitments, challenges, and the things you want to build across every domain of your life, before any of it gets ranked.

<!-- truncate -->

## What do you want to do?

- What have I thought about doing?
- What are my different streams of work?
- What should I do with my time?
- What's the most valuable thing I can do now?
- How can I make the most out of my time?
- What initiatives do I have?
- Why do I think each of these initiatives is important?
- What must be done in order to realize my goals?
- How many tasks do I have in each objective?

A note on capturing goals: record your goals in the context of your roles.

## Goals by category

### Career

- What are my career goals?
- What skills do I want to attain?
- What position do I want to reach?

### Family

- What are my family goals?
- What do I want to accomplish for my family?

### Financial

- What are my financial goals?
- What financial milestones do I want to reach?

### Growth

- How do I want to grow as a person?
- What aspects of myself do I want to improve?
- Do I need to improve these aspects of me on a daily basis?
- If so, what recurring action do I need to take for each aspect?
- What sets of things should I truly currently be focusing on?

### Health

- What are my health goals?
- How do I want to improve my physical well-being?

### Learning

- What do I want to learn?
- What skills do I need to develop?
- Of the things I could possibly learn about, what have I chosen to focus on?

### Spiritual

- What are my spiritual goals?
- How do I want to grow spiritually?

## Commitments

- What are my commitments?
- What have I physically committed my time to doing?
- Do my commitments reflect my priorities?
- How does this differ from my goals?
- What commitments have I made that will stick with me until I die?
- Are initiatives the same as commitments?
- When did I make each of my commitments?
- Am I keeping up with each of my commitments?
- Which of my personal goals do I attempt to partially realize through my commitments?
- How long does each of my commitments last for?
- How much of my daily time does each commitment consume?
- What are my annual commitments?
- What commitments have I made to my family?
- What commitments have I made to myself?
- What commitments have I made spiritually?

## Challenges

- How will I challenge myself?
- How am I challenging myself?
- What will I push myself to do?
- What am I motivated to do?
- What difficult things do I want to accomplish?
- What challenges am I facing?
- What milestones seem hard to reach?

You need to consistently create new challenges for yourself to keep yourself engaged.

## Things to build (artifactual outcomes)

- What do I want to build/create?
- What artifacts do I want to produce?
- What apps, sites, diagrams, or documents do I want to make?

## Themes

- What themes scale across my initiatives?
- On what basis do I have ideas? Where do my ideas come from?

## Why nots

- Why don't I...?
- What am I NOT doing that I should consider?

## Reflecting on your initiatives

- What goals are hidden in my initiatives?
- Why do I want to do each of these things?
- As time has progressed, how are the things I want to work on changing?

## Identifying projects

- What projects can I potentially work on?
- What bundle of work can I do?
- What is the end result / artifact / change in state of each project?
- Why should I work on each project?
- How do I go from a project idea to a work bundle?
- How can I refine my ideas / learning objectives into projects?
- Part of innovating: I have an idea to do something that, when merged with things I want to do or am passionate about, becomes a project bundle I can work on.

Reflecting on completed projects:

- What objectives were advanced by the projects I have already completed?
- What objectives have I advanced through my projects?
