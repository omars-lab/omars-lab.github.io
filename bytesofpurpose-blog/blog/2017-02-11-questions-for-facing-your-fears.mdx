Fear rarely announces itself honestly. It hides behind hesitation, busyness, and good excuses. These are the questions I return to when I want to drag a fear into the open and see it clearly, grouped by what they're really asking. Sit with the ones that make you flinch.

<!-- truncate -->

## Core fears

<SectionBanner why="Most fear runs in the background, unnamed. These questions force me to say out loud what I'm actually afraid of, because a fear I can name is one I can finally examine." />

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="core"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="deep"
  why="A fear stays in control as long as it stays vague. Naming exactly what I'm afraid of strips away its power to run me from the shadows, and it's the first move in every other question here."
  howOften="Whenever hesitation shows up without a clear reason"
  when="When I notice I'm avoiding something and don't quite know why"
  record="Write the fear in one plain sentence, with no euphemism">
  What are you afraid of?
</Question>

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="high"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="deep"
  why="Some fears don't just scare me, they steer me. Identifying the ones actively holding me back separates the harmless fears from the ones quietly shrinking my life."
  howOften="When I feel stuck or smaller than I want to be"
  when="When I catch myself not pursuing something I clearly want"
  record="List the fears that are costing you specific opportunities">
  What fears are holding you back?
</Question>

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="high"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="deep"
  why="Most fear is really fear of loss. Naming what I'm afraid to lose (status, safety, an identity, a person) exposes what I'm actually protecting, which is usually more telling than the fear itself."
  howOften="When a fear feels disproportionate to the situation"
  when="When I'm clinging to something harder than it deserves"
  record="Write what you're afraid to lose, then ask if it's really at risk">
  What do you fear losing?
</Question>

<Question
  power={['fire', 'chisel']}
  priority="high"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="deep"
  why="There's a difference between moving toward something and fleeing from something. Asking what I'm running from catches the avoidance dressed up as motion, which is the hardest fear to see."
  howOften="When I'm unusually busy but not at peace"
  when="When my drive feels more like escape than pursuit"
  record="Name what you're avoiding and what you'd have to face if you stopped running">
  What are you running from?
</Question>

## What threatens you

<SectionBanner why="Once I know my fears, I look at what's actually behind them. These questions separate real threats from imagined ones so I can spend my courage where it counts." />

<Question
  power={['fire', 'anvil']}
  priority="medium"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="deep"
  why="Once the fear of dying loses its grip, almost every smaller threat shrinks too. This question recalibrates the whole hierarchy of what I let scare me."
  howOften="Rarely, during deep reflection on mortality"
  when="After a brush with loss, or in a season of unusual courage"
  record="Write what still threatens you once the biggest fear is set aside">
  When you no longer fear dying, what else can threaten you?
</Question>

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="medium"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Naming my opposition makes it concrete instead of looming. A clearly identified obstacle is something I can strategize against; a vague dread is not."
  howOften="When facing resistance to a goal"
  when="When something or someone is standing between me and where I'm trying to go"
  record="Name the specific opposition, person, force, or circumstance">
  What opposition do you face?
</Question>

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="high"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Knowing what threatens my goals lets me defend them on purpose. Most dreams don't die from one big attack; they erode from threats I never named and so never guarded against."
  howOften="When setting or reviewing a serious goal"
  when="When I want to protect something I care about achieving"
  record="List the real threats to your goals and one guard for each">
  What threatens your goals and dreams?
</Question>

## Overcoming fear

<SectionBanner why="Naming fear is only half the work. This cluster is about the move from fear to action: what dissolves the fear, what I'd do without it, and how I pressure myself to grow through it." />

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="high"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Fear of opposition usually rests on a few specific beliefs. Finding what actually removes that fear (preparation, faith, a reframe) gives me something to reach for instead of just bracing."
  howOften="When facing a specific opposition"
  when="When fear of an obstacle is keeping me from acting"
  record="Note what reliably shrinks your fear, then use it deliberately">
  What removes your fear of your opposition?
</Question>

<Question
  power={['fire', 'anvil']}
  priority="medium"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="deep"
  why="Pressure is what turns coal into diamond. Reframing fear as the pressure that forms me changes it from something to escape into something to use, as long as I choose the pressure on purpose."
  howOften="When I want to grow through a hard thing rather than around it"
  when="When facing a challenge that could either crush me or make me"
  record="Write how you'll apply pressure intentionally instead of letting it just happen">
  How do you pressure yourself to become a diamond?
</Question>

<Question
  power={['fire', 'spark']}
  priority="core"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="deep"
  why="This is the question that cuts straight to it. Imagining what I'd do without the fear reveals what I actually want, and exposes how much of my current path is shaped by avoidance rather than desire."
  howOften="At any real decision point clouded by fear"
  when="When I'm choosing and can feel fear tilting the scale"
  record="Write what you'd do fearlessly, then ask what's truly stopping you">
  What would you do if you weren't afraid?
</Question>

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="high"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Fear left alone just loops. The move from fear to action is a skill, and asking how I make that move turns a paralyzing feeling into a concrete next step."
  howOften="Whenever fear has me frozen"
  when="When I've sat in fear long enough and need to break out of it"
  record="Write the smallest action that moves you from frozen to moving">
  How do you move from fear to action?
</Question>

## Courage and faith

<SectionBanner why="Courage isn't the absence of fear, it's having something stronger to draw on. These questions find the source of my strength so I can reach for it when sight runs out." />

<Question
  priority="high"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Courage isn't a constant; it has sources. Knowing what gives me courage (a person, a purpose, a belief) lets me return to the well deliberately instead of hoping bravery shows up on its own."
  howOften="When I need to act despite fear"
  when="Before facing something that frightens me"
  record="Name your sources of courage and keep them close">
  What gives you courage?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="medium"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Strength to continue is different from courage to start. Naming what keeps me going under sustained pressure tells me what to lean on when the fear is a long grind rather than a single leap."
  howOften="In long, hard stretches"
  when="When I'm worn down and tempted to quit"
  record="Write what keeps you moving when it would be easier to stop">
  What gives you strength to keep going?
</Question>

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="medium"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="deep"
  why="Acting without guarantees is the heart of facing fear. Walking by faith and not by sight is the practice of moving forward when I can't see the outcome, which is exactly when fear is loudest."
  howOften="When the path forward is uncertain"
  when="When I have to commit before I can see how it turns out"
  record="Note where you're demanding certainty before you'll move, and act anyway">
  How do you walk by faith and not by sight?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="medium"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Trusting the process is what lets me keep working when results aren't visible yet. Knowing what helps me trust it keeps fear of wasted effort from pulling me off course early."
  howOften="When progress feels invisible"
  when="When I'm tempted to abandon something because I can't yet see it working"
  record="Write what helps you trust the process when doubt creeps in">
  What helps you trust the process?
</Question>

## Facing opposition

<SectionBanner why="Opposition, rejection, and setbacks are where fear becomes real and unavoidable. These questions prepare me to meet them on purpose instead of being caught off guard." />

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="high"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="moderate"
  why="How I face opposition determines whether it stops me or sharpens me. Having a deliberate way to meet resistance keeps me from defaulting to flight or freeze in the moment."
  howOften="When opposition shows up"
  when="When I'm actively up against a person or force resisting me"
  record="Write your approach to opposition before you're in the heat of it">
  How do you face your opposition?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="medium"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Adversity is far less frightening when I've prepared for it. Asking what prepares me converts a vague dread of hard times into a concrete readiness I can build in advance."
  howOften="During calm stretches, in preparation"
  when="Before entering a season I expect to be difficult"
  record="List what you can do now to be ready for the adversity you can see coming">
  What prepares you for adversity?
</Question>

<Question
  power={['fire', 'chisel']}
  priority="high"
  cron="adhoc"
  depth="deep"
  why="Fear of rejection stops more action than rejection itself ever does. Building a real way to handle rejection and setbacks defuses that fear, because I know I can survive the thing I'm dreading."
  howOften="When risking rejection"
  when="After a rejection or setback, and before risking another"
  record="Write how you recover from a no, so the fear of one loses its grip">
  How do you handle rejection and setbacks?
</Question>
