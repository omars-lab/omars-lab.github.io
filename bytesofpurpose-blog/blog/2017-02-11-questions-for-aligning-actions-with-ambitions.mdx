Ambitions are easy to declare and hard to live up to. The gap between the two shows
up in what I actually do all day. These are the questions I use to close that gap: to
notice where my actions drift from what I claim to want, and to decide what to stop,
what to start, and what to double down on. I pick the ones that make me a little
uncomfortable.

<!-- truncate -->

## Action-ambition alignment

<SectionBanner why="My calendar tells the truth my intentions don't. These questions hold my daily actions up against my stated ambitions and name the gap, which is the whole reason the rest of the work exists." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power={['fire', 'anvil']}
  priority="core"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="deep"
  why="This is the whole question in one line: do the things I actually do line up with what I say I want? Most of the gap between ambition and outcome lives here, and naming it is the first honest step to closing it."
  howOften="Monthly, as the core alignment check"
  when="When I feel busy but not like I'm moving toward what I claim to want"
  record="Write a plain yes or no, then the single biggest place my actions and ambitions diverge">
  Do your actions match your ambitions?
</Question>

<Question
  power="chisel"
  priority="core"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="deep"
  why="Laying my current actions next to my ambitions makes the misalignment visible instead of vaguely felt. Seeing them side by side turns 'something's off' into a specific gap I can actually act on."
  howOften="Monthly, alongside the question above"
  when="When I want to locate exactly where my effort and my aims part ways"
  record="Two columns, actions and ambitions; draw lines between matches and circle the orphans on each side">
  What are your current actions, and what are your ambitions? Where is the misalignment? What actions actually align with your ambitions?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="high"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Alignment is diagnosis; this is the prescription. Naming what I'm actually going to do to succeed turns the gap I've found into a concrete plan instead of just a clearer picture of being stuck."
  howOften="Monthly, after diagnosing the misalignment"
  when="Once I've seen the gap and need to commit to closing it"
  record="Write the specific actions I'll take to succeed, with the first one starting this week">
  What are you going to do to be successful?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## What to stop doing

<SectionBanner why="Progress is often subtraction, not addition. The quiet 'why do I have to do this?' that surfaces when I'm resisting something is a useful signal that it may need to stop, so these questions hunt for what to cut." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power="spark"
  priority="high"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="That flash of 'why do I have to do this?' is data, not just complaint. The resistance often points at something that doesn't serve my ambitions and should be cut, so this question reads the resistance instead of ignoring it."
  howOften="Monthly, and any time that resentment flares up"
  when="When I catch myself resisting a task and want to know what the resistance is telling me"
  record="Note what triggered the resistance, then ask whether the task actually serves anything I want">
  Why do you have to do this? What does that resistance tell you?
</Question>

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="high"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Naming what I do that doesn't serve my ambitions, and then committing to stop it, is the highest-leverage move I can make, because every hour freed is an hour returned to what matters. Identifying the dead weight is harder than it sounds; it usually hides as 'normal.'"
  howOften="Monthly, as a deliberate subtraction pass"
  when="When my days are full but my ambitions aren't moving"
  record="List what doesn't serve my ambitions, then pick the one thing I'll actually stop this month">
  What are you doing that doesn't serve your ambitions? What should you stop doing?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## What to start doing

<SectionBanner why="The most useful starting questions begin with 'why,' because they surface the thing I keep avoiding. These questions name what I've been putting off and turn it into something I'll actually begin." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power={['fire', 'chisel']}
  priority="high"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="deep"
  why="Stacking 'why haven't you,' 'why can't you,' and 'why don't you' wears down the excuse until the real reason shows. The thing I keep avoiding is usually the thing that would move me most, which is exactly why I avoid it."
  howOften="Monthly, when something keeps not getting started"
  when="When I've been meaning to begin something and keep not"
  record="Answer each 'why' honestly; the answer I least want to write is the one to act on">
  Why haven't you done it? Why can't you? Why don't you?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="high"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Once I know what I've been avoiding, this names the concrete thing to begin that would actually close the gap. Starting something new is how alignment becomes forward motion instead of just a cleaner diagnosis."
  howOften="Monthly, paired with the avoidance question above"
  when="When I've surfaced what I'm avoiding and need to commit to starting it"
  record="Write the one new thing I'll start, and the specific first action that begins it">
  What should you start doing to align with your ambitions?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## What to increase doing

<SectionBanner why="Alignment isn't only about stopping and starting; it's also about feeding what's already working. These questions find the things I should simply do more of, especially the gift-developing ones that need more time than they get." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  priority="medium"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="quick"
  why="The things already working that I should do more of are the easiest wins, since the hard part of starting is done. The trap is that I under-feed them while chasing new things, so this question protects what's already paying off."
  howOften="Monthly, as a quick amplification check"
  when="When I want to grow what's working instead of only fixing what isn't"
  record="Name what's working and deserves more of me; block real time for it next month">
  What are you doing that you should do more of?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="high"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Some aligned activities are starved of the time and energy they need to matter. Naming them is how I move them from 'nice when I get to it' to a real, protected slot, because alignment without time invested is just good intentions."
  howOften="Monthly, when allocating my time"
  when="When something that genuinely serves my ambitions keeps getting squeezed out"
  record="List the under-fed aligned activities; assign each a concrete time and energy budget">
  What activities align with your ambitions but need more time and energy?
</Question>

<Question
  power="anvil"
  priority="high"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="deep"
  why="Fully using my talents and developing my gifts is where doing more compounds over time. Asking it directly keeps me from leaving my best abilities idle while I stay busy with lesser things, which is the most expensive kind of misalignment."
  howOften="Quarterly, as a stewardship check"
  when="When I notice a strength I keep neglecting while busy elsewhere"
  record="Write whether I'm fully using my talents, then one concrete way I'll develop a neglected gift">
  How will you fully utilize your talents? Are you developing your gifts?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Challenging yourself

<SectionBanner why="Alignment without stretch turns into a comfortable rut. These questions check whether I'm actually at the edge of my comfort zone, and whether the pressure I'm putting on myself is the kind that forms a diamond or just wears me down." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="high"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="If I'm not at the edge of my comfort zone, I'm probably coasting, and coasting feels like alignment but isn't growth. This question checks whether my actions are actually stretching me toward my ambitions or just keeping me comfortable."
  howOften="Monthly, as a stretch check"
  when="When things feel a little too easy and I suspect I've stopped growing"
  record="Answer honestly whether I'm at my edge; if not, name the next thing that would put me there">
  Are you challenging yourself? Are you at the edge of your comfort zone? How are you challenging yourself?
</Question>

<Question
  power={['fire', 'chisel']}
  priority="medium"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="deep"
  why="Where I fall short of my own expectations is where my self-knowledge sharpens fastest. The pressure-into-a-diamond framing reminds me that the right kind of pressure forms me, while the wrong kind just grinds, and the why behind the shortfall tells which it is."
  howOften="Quarterly, when reviewing where I missed my own mark"
  when="When I've fallen short of what I expected of myself and want to understand why"
  record="Write where and why I fell short; decide whether the fix is more pressure or a kinder, smarter kind">
  Where and why do you fall short of your expectations? How do you pressure yourself to become a diamond?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Progress and purpose alignment

<SectionBanner why="It's possible to be busy, even challenged, and still drifting away from my purpose. These questions zoom out to ask whether all the motion is actually forward, and whether it's pointed at what I'm here to do." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power={['fire', 'anvil']}
  priority="core"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="deep"
  why="Actions can align with my ambitions and still miss my purpose, which is the deeper alignment. This question checks the highest level: not just whether I'm productive, but whether the productivity is pointed at what I'm actually here to do."
  howOften="Quarterly, as the deepest alignment check"
  when="When my work is going well but feels disconnected from why I started"
  record="Write whether the work serves my purpose; if not, name what's pulling it off course">
  Is what you are doing in tune with your purpose?
</Question>

<Question
  power={['chisel', 'anvil']}
  priority="high"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="deep"
  why="The stop-X-start-Y question makes the cost of misalignment vivid by naming who I could become if I traded the wrong activity for the right one. It turns a vague sense of waste into a concrete, motivating swap."
  howOften="Quarterly, when weighing a real trade"
  when="When I sense one activity is crowding out something far more worthwhile"
  record="Name the specific X to drop and Y to take up, and the version of me that swap creates">
  What could you be if you stopped doing X and started doing Y?
</Question>

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="medium"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Motion and progress aren't the same; I can be active and still slipping backward. Asking whether I'm moving forward or back, progressing or stagnating, cuts through the comfort of mere busyness to ask if it's actually getting me anywhere."
  howOften="Monthly, as a direction check"
  when="When I'm active but unsure whether I'm actually advancing"
  record="Answer plainly forward or back, progressing or stagnant, and the evidence for which">
  Are you moving forward, or moving back? Are you making progress, or are you stagnated?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Effort and values

<SectionBanner why="Alignment has two tests: am I giving it everything I've got, and does what I'm doing actually reflect what I believe? These questions check both my effort and my integrity at once." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="high"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="'All you can with what you have' is the effort test, stripped of excuses about what I lack. It asks whether I'm fully using my actual circumstances rather than waiting for better ones, which is where most wasted potential hides."
  howOften="Monthly, as an honest effort check"
  when="When I'm tempted to blame my circumstances for what I haven't done"
  record="Write whether I'm truly using what I have, then the one resource I'm leaving idle">
  Are you doing all you can with what you have?
</Question>

<Question
  power={['fire', 'chisel']}
  priority="core"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="deep"
  why="Actions that clash with my values create a quiet friction that drains me even when I'm succeeding. This is the integrity test of alignment: not just whether my actions serve my ambitions, but whether they honor what I actually believe."
  howOften="Monthly, as an integrity check"
  when="When I feel an unease about how I'm achieving something, even though it's working"
  record="Name where my actions and values clash; decide what to change so they stop fighting">
  Do your actions resonate with your values?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Intentions

<SectionBanner why="Before action comes intention, and a muddy intention produces scattered action. Naming clearly what I intend to do is what gives everything downstream a direction to point at." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power="spark"
  priority="medium"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Stating my intentions plainly is what separates deliberate action from drift. A clear intention is the seed every aligned action grows from, so naming it precisely keeps the rest of the day from running on autopilot."
  howOften="Monthly, and at the start of anything significant"
  when="When I'm about to begin something and want it pointed on purpose"
  record="Write the intention in one clear sentence; check later whether my actions actually carried it out">
  What are your intentions? What do you intend to do?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Taking action

<SectionBanner why="All the reflection only matters if it ends in a move. These questions close the loop: naming the concrete thing I'll do toward my goals and the plan that carries it into tomorrow." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power="anvil"
  priority="high"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="This converts everything above into a committed move, because alignment that never reaches action is just well-organized wishing. Naming exactly what I'll do to achieve my goals is where reflection finally becomes momentum."
  howOften="Monthly, as the closing commitment"
  when="At the end of any alignment review, before I let myself stop"
  record="Write the specific actions toward my goals, each with a date or trigger attached">
  What will you do to achieve your goals?
</Question>

<Question
  power="chisel"
  priority="high"
  cron="weekly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Change has a price, and a plan for tomorrow is how I actually pay it instead of admiring the idea. Asking what it takes keeps me honest about the cost, and turns 'I should change' into a concrete plan I can start on in the morning."
  howOften="Weekly, when setting up the next stretch"
  when="When I've decided to change something and need it to survive contact with tomorrow"
  record="Write tomorrow's specific plan and the one habit or choice the change actually requires">
  What does it take to change, to set a plan for tomorrow?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>
