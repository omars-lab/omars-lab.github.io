Growth starts with an honest map of where I stand. Before I can pick what to work on, I have to name the gaps, notice the openings, and decide what change I actually want. These are the questions I return to when I want to figure out where to improve next.

<!-- truncate -->

## How to use these

<SectionBanner why="These work best in rough order: first surface gaps and weaknesses, then spot growth opportunities, then prioritize what's most valuable and aligned with my goals, and finally plan how I'll actually improve. The sections below mirror those steps." />

## Identifying gaps and weaknesses

<SectionBanner why="I can't fix what I won't name. This section is the honest inventory of where I'm weak, including a concrete checklist of specific areas to rate myself against so the gaps can't hide behind vagueness." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="high"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="deep"
  why="Naming weaknesses out loud is uncomfortable, which is exactly why it works. Asking where I can improve, what my weaknesses are, and which ones I need to work on turns a vague unease into a list I can act on."
  howOften="Quarterly"
  when="During an honest self-assessment"
  record="Write the weaknesses plainly; mark the ones that need work now">
  Where can I improve? What are my weaknesses? What weaknesses of mine do I need to work on?
</Question>

<Question
  priority="medium"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Weaknesses only matter relative to where I'm trying to go. Lining up my goals against what I'm not yet ready for shows me which gaps actually block the path."
  howOften="Quarterly"
  when="When tying gaps back to goals"
  record="List your goals, then note what you're not ready for yet">
  What are my goals? What am I not ready for?
</Question>

<Question
  power="anvil"
  priority="medium"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="deep"
  why="A concrete checklist beats a vague sense of inadequacy. Running down specific areas and answering honestly where I'm weak turns self-criticism into a precise, actionable map."
  howOften="Quarterly"
  when="When I need a concrete, specific gap inventory"
  record="Rate yourself honestly on each area; circle the weakest few to target">
  Run down a checklist of specific areas and ask honestly where you are weak: Scaling? Performance? Security (SSL/TLS, vulnerability scanning, OAuth)? Agile? Business models? Product offerings? Web dev beyond the basics (CSS)? Experience with databases? Can't show my work (no repos, no docs)? Design, color, palette? Cloud development? Functional programming?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Growth opportunities

<SectionBanner why="Gaps tell me where I'm behind; opportunities tell me where I could stretch. This section looks forward, toward the new and uncomfortable, because growth lives where I'm willing to be a beginner." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power="spark"
  priority="medium"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Growth happens at the edge of comfort. Asking how I can evolve, what new things I can do, and where I can accept being uncomfortable points me toward the stretch instead of the safe."
  howOften="Quarterly"
  when="When looking for the next place to stretch"
  record="Name a new thing to try and the discomfort you'll accept">
  How can I evolve? What new things can I do? Where can I accept being uncomfortable? What should I push myself to do?
</Question>

<Question
  power="anvil"
  priority="high"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="deep"
  why="One-off growth is easy; a mindset that always grows is the real goal. Asking how I establish that mindset, anchored by simple commitments to always read and always study, turns growth from an event into a default."
  howOften="Quarterly"
  when="When building the habit of continuous growth"
  record="Write the mindset and the standing habits, like always read and always study, that keep momentum">
  How do I ensure I always grow? How do I establish a mindset that pushes me to always grow?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Desiring change

<SectionBanner why="Improvement starts with actually wanting it. This short section checks whether I have a real desire to change, because effort without genuine want rarely sticks." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power="chisel"
  priority="medium"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="deep"
  why="Change requires desire, not just diagnosis. Naming the change I desire and what about myself I want to change is the difference between knowing my flaws and actually moving on them."
  howOften="Quarterly"
  when="When deciding what about myself to change"
  record="Write the change you genuinely want, in your own words">
  What change do I desire? What about myself do I want to change?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Learning from others

<SectionBanner why="Other people are a mirror for my own gaps. Spotting skills in them that I lack is one of the fastest ways to see what I should work on, and who I should learn it from." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  priority="medium"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="The skills I admire in others are often the ones I'm missing. Naming those, and who I can learn each from, turns vague envy into a concrete learning plan."
  howOften="Monthly"
  when="When I notice a skill in someone I wish I had"
  record="List skills you've spotted in others that you lack; note who to learn each from">
  What skills have I spotted in others that I lack myself? Who can I learn from?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Your improvement approach

<SectionBanner why="Knowing where to improve is useless without a plan to do it. This section turns the gaps into a concrete approach: the actions, the habits, and how it all ties back to my goals and learning." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  priority="high"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="An approach is what makes improvement happen on purpose. Asking how I'll improve, what I need to do, and how it overlaps with my goals and learning keeps the effort coherent instead of scattered."
  howOften="Quarterly"
  when="When turning gaps into an actual plan"
  record="Write the improvement actions and how each overlaps with your goals and learning">
  How will I improve? What do I need to do to improve? How does this overlap with my goals? How does this overlap with things I want to learn?
</Question>

<Question
  power="anvil"
  priority="high"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="deep"
  why="Improvement compounds over years, and habits are the mechanism. Planning how I'll improve this year and next, what I'll learn, and which habits I'll start, stop, and commit to is what makes the change durable."
  howOften="Yearly"
  when="During annual planning of growth and habits"
  record="Write what you'll learn and the habits you'll start, stop, and commit to">
  How will I improve this year? Next year? What things will I learn? What habits will I start or stop? What habits am I willing to commit to?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Technical gaps

<SectionBanner why="Beyond general growth, there are specific technical things I want to understand better. This section names the concrete frameworks, tools, and processes on my list, like rasa, spaCy, Google Analytics, Spark, the Critical Path Method, and a Gantt-style view for ordering work." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  priority="medium"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Naming specific frameworks and processes turns 'get better technically' into something I can actually schedule. Concrete targets, like the frameworks and process enhancements on my own list, are learnable; vague ambition isn't."
  howOften="Quarterly"
  when="When choosing the next technical thing to learn"
  record="List the frameworks, tools, and processes you want to understand better">
  What frameworks do I want to learn more about? What processes do I need to understand better?
</Question>

<Question
  power="fire"
  priority="high"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="deep"
  why="The underlying skillset is where the deepest gaps usually live. Admitting I need to get better at architecting, at understanding the phases of software development, and at the tools at my disposal is the kind of honesty that actually directs growth."
  howOften="Quarterly"
  when="When assessing core skills, not just surface frameworks"
  record="Name the foundational skills that need work, like architecting and understanding the development phases">
  Which skills of mine need work?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Expanding your roles

<SectionBanner why="Growth isn't only about getting better at what I already do; it's about taking on new activities and roles. This section looks at widening the kinds of things I do, not just deepening them." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power="spark"

  cron="quarterly"

</QuestionSection>

## Enhancing your skills

<SectionBanner why="Some of the most valuable skills are the soft and structural ones I tend to neglect: persuasion, negotiation, showing my value, and modeling cleanly. This section targets those underinvested capabilities." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  priority="medium"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="Influence skills, swaying people, negotiating, showcasing my value, often matter more than technical depth and get practiced less. Naming them as things to improve keeps me from neglecting the skills that actually move outcomes."
  howOften="Quarterly"
  when="When working on the people-facing side of my skillset"
  record="Note where you want to improve at persuasion, negotiation, and showing your value">
  How do I sway people? How do I negotiate? How do I showcase my value?
</Question>

<Question
  power="chisel"
  priority="low"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="How I model and where I spend attention shapes the quality of my work. Asking whether I can model in ADTs more and focus less on processes and more on capabilities sharpens how I actually think and build."
  howOften="Quarterly"
  when="When refining how I model and where I focus"
  record="Note where you can lean more on ADTs and focus on capabilities over processes">
  Can I model in ADTs as much as possible? Can I focus less on processes and more on capabilities?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>
