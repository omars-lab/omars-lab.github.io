This is an early, evolving catalog of the questions I find worth answering. It is more raw and aphoristic than my later question sets, and that is the point: it is a place to gather what I am curious about, figure out where I need to understand more, and consolidate the questions worth pursuing as part of a larger quest. Treat it as a living list, not a finished argument.

<!-- truncate -->

## Value

<SectionBanner why="Value is where attention and money actually flow, and following that flow is the surest way to find what people genuinely care about. If I can read where value pools, I can read what to build." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power="spark"
  priority="high"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="moderate"
  why="The places people willingly spend time and money are a map of real demand. Most of my product instincts are downstream of reading that map correctly."
  howOften="Quarterly, and whenever I am evaluating a new direction"
  when="When deciding what to build, what to study, or where to focus effort"
  record="Keep a running note of where you observe time and money concentrating, with a concrete example each time">
  Where are people more likely to spend their time and money?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Avoiding Failure

<SectionBanner why="Knowing why people fail is cheaper than learning it firsthand. If failure usually traces back to a missing resource, then resourcefulness becomes the thing worth cultivating." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power="anvil"
  priority="high"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="deep"
  why="If I can name the common causes of failure ahead of time, I can design around them instead of walking into them. A frequent root cause is the lack of some critical resource."
  howOften="Quarterly, and after any setback worth a post-mortem"
  when="Before committing to something risky, and when reviewing why a past effort stalled"
  record="List the failure modes you observe, and tag each with the resource that was missing">

</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Spending

<SectionBanner why="How people spend money and time is the most honest record of what they value. If I can read that record, even from something as ordinary as the house someone wants, I can understand people better." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power="spark"
  priority="high"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="deep"
  why="Spending is revealed preference. What people pour money and time into tells me what they actually want, regardless of what they say. Even a home someone is drawn to leaks personal attributes through its features."
  howOften="Quarterly, and whenever you study a market or an audience"
  when="When trying to understand what an audience truly cares about"
  record="Collect specific examples of spending patterns and what they imply about the person">
  What do people spend most of their money on? Their time? Why do people spend their money? Their time? Can you infer personal attributes about someone from a home they are interested in, based on the different features of the house? Where are people spending their time?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Planning

<SectionBanner why="Planning is one of the deepest questions in this catalog: who mastered it, whether it even matters more than direction, and how granular it should be. I want to understand the craft of planning at every scale, from a person to a country." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power={['anvil', 'chisel']}
  priority="high"
  cron="quarterly"
  depth="deep"
  why="Planning scales strangely. The same instinct that organizes a person can, in principle, organize a country, and history is full of people who mastered it. I want to know when planning helps, when mere direction is enough, and when granularity tips into paralysis."
  howOften="Quarterly, and whenever you set out to build a plan"
  when="When putting together any plan, for yourself or something larger"
  record="Study planners you admire; extract the principles, and note where you are being too granular">
  How do you put together a plan for yourself? How do you put a plan together for an entire country? How can you extract someone's plan from history? Who mastered planning? Is planning needed, or direction? What level of planning is needed to be successful? Are you being too granular?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Questions on Questions

<SectionBanner why="This catalog is itself a system, and a system needs maintenance. These are the meta-questions about how to organize questions, whether they inherit from each other, and whether the set paints a complete picture without repeating itself." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power={['chisel', 'anvil']}
  priority="high"
  cron="monthly"
  depth="deep"
  why="If questions are my unit of work, then organizing them well is leverage. I want to know whether they can inherit from each other, how to map them to their essential contexts, whether the set is complete, and whether I am asking the same thing twice."
  howOften="Monthly, as part of maintaining this catalog"
  when="When the question list grows unwieldy or starts to feel redundant"
  record="Define your taxonomy: how questions relate, what contexts they map to, and which are ready to answer">
  How do I organize my questions? Can questions inherit from each other? On what basis? How do I map questions to their essential contexts? What contexts am I tracking? Are the dependencies of the contexts the same as the dependencies of the questions? Do your questions paint a complete picture? Are you asking the same thing twice? What questions are you ready to answer? What do you need to do to be able to come up with an answer? What questions should you always ask yourself? What are the questions you need to ask yourself?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>

## Inference

<SectionBanner why="Inference is where credibility lives. I want to know how collective inference works, how accurate ours is, and where I want my word to be trusted, because the questions I dedicate my life to answering will only matter if people believe the answers." />

<QuestionSection>

<Question
  power={['fire', 'anvil']}
  priority="core"
  cron="yearly"
  depth="deep"
  why="These are the deepest questions in the catalog: how inference is amplified or reduced, what counts as evidence, whether I am even qualified to answer, and which questions I want to spend the rest of my life on. They decide where my credibility is staked."
  howOften="Yearly, as long-horizon questions that should refine slowly"
  when="During deep reflection on what to commit your life to answering"
  record="Write the questions you want to be trusted on, and what you must know to earn that trust">
  How do you do collective inference? On what basis is a possible inference amplified or reduced? How accurate is our inference? Where do you want your word to be trusted? Where do you want to be credible? Into what will you put your time? What kinds of questions do you want to answer for the rest of your life? What will we do for evidence? Am I qualified to answer this question? What must I know to answer this question?
</Question>

</QuestionSection>
