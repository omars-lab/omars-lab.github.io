Growth starts with an honest map of where you stand. Before you can pick what to work on, you have to name the gaps, notice the openings, and decide what change you actually want. These are the questions I return to when I want to figure out where to improve next.

<!-- truncate -->

## How to use these

Work through these in rough order: first surface your gaps and weaknesses, then spot growth opportunities, then prioritize what is most valuable and aligned with your goals, and finally plan how you will actually improve. The questions in each section below mirror those steps.

## Identifying gaps and weaknesses

- Where can I improve?
- What are my weaknesses?
- What weaknesses of mine do I need to work on?
- What are my goals?
- What am I not ready for?

A more concrete pass can help. Run down a checklist of specific areas and ask honestly where you are weak:

- Scaling?
- Performance?
- Security (SSL/TLS, vulnerability scanning, OAuth)?
- Agile?
- Business models?
- Product offerings?
- Web dev beyond the basics (CSS)?
- Experience with databases?
- Can't show my work (no repos, no docs)?
- Design, color, palette?
- Cloud development?
- Functional programming?

## Growth opportunities

- How can I evolve?
- What new things can I do?
- Where can I accept being uncomfortable?
- What should I push myself to do?

And to keep that momentum going:

- How do I ensure I always grow?
- How do I establish a mindset that pushes me to always grow?
- Always read.
- Always study.

## Desiring change

- What change do I desire?
- What about myself do I want to change?

## Learning from others

- What skills have I spotted in others that I lack myself?
- Who can I learn from?

## Your improvement approach

- How will I improve?
- What do I need to do to improve?
- How does this overlap with my goals?
- How does this overlap with things I want to learn?
- How will I improve this year? Next year?
- What things will I learn?
- What habits will I start or stop?
- What habits am I willing to commit to?

## Technical gaps

- What frameworks do I want to learn more about?
- What processes do I need to understand better?

Some example targets I have kept on my own list:

- Frameworks and tools to learn: rasa, spaCy, Google Analytics, Spark.
- Process enhancements: the Critical Path Method, and a graph or Gantt-style view for prioritizing what needs to be done first.

And for the underlying skillset:

- Which skills of mine need work?
- I need to get better at architecting.
- I need to understand the phases of software development.
- I need to understand the different tools at my disposal.

## Expanding your roles

- What new activities should I take on?
- What new roles should I play?

## Enhancing your skills

- How do I sway people?
- How do I negotiate?
- How do I showcase my value?
- Can I model in ADTs as much as possible?
- Can I focus less on processes and more on capabilities?
