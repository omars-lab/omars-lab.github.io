Starting a business is partly a checklist of questions and partly a mindset. The questions force you to get specific about what you are offering, who it is for, and how it reaches them. The mindset is what keeps you moving when the questions get hard. Here is both.

A few framing thoughts on how businesses actually work:

- Businesses consider requests for proof of the value you add.
- Great magic makes you careful about what you believe in.
- Business and sales are like a magic trick. It is all about perception.
- Should product management and engineers have a tense relationship? Why?

Some concrete next steps if you go this route:

- Figure out your personal products.
- Figure out what people are most likely to spend their resources on.
- Figure out how to be more resourceful.
- Read on: convincing, making a product, starting a business, pleasing others, and behavior.

One pattern worth noticing: people make money off making something easy to use. Tools like Sheetsu and Microsoft Flow are simple examples of turning friction into a product.

## The mindset to keep going

<SectionBanner why="The questions above are the easy part; following through is harder. This is the material I come back to when I need to keep going, kept as prose because it is meant to be read and felt, not answered." />

The questions above are the easy part. Following through is harder. Here is the material I come back to when I need to keep going.

### It's possible

> Your dream is possible. Others have done it, so why can't you?

You can live your dream. Choose your future, and live it.

### It's necessary

Improvement is not optional. Work on yourself, develop yourself, and keep asking: how can I improve this? How can I improve the lives of those around me?

Remove the energy drainers. People who stopped working on themselves, who stopped dreaming, will pull you down. Get the losers out of your life. Then align yourself with people who attract you upward: people who are unstoppable, unreasonable, unwilling to live life just as it is, and who want more.

Know this:

- People like to complain, but they won't do anything about their situation.
- You are an uncommon breed. Not everybody will see it, join you, or share the vision, and that is fine.
- I can do this. Even though people don't see it for me, I can see it for myself.

Understand the importance of adjusting your strategy. Become creative. Be flexible. Be a good model of truth, integrity, and commitment. Surround yourself with winners.

> If not you, then who? If not now, then when?

You are the author of your own story. Setbacks are part of the path. Review your dream every day, and remember: it's not over until I win.

### It's me

No one can do it but you.

- Have goals, make a plan, and think constantly about how you can improve.
- Take personal responsibility. Make it your personal business to succeed, and to come through when everyone is doubting you.

### Face your fears

Run toward your dream. Work toward it. Act on your ideas. Life is about stretching, challenging yourself, and working on ways to improve.

In the process you will incur disappointment, failure, pain, setbacks, and defeats. But you will also discover that you have greatness within you, that you are more powerful than you can begin to imagine, that you are greater than your circumstances, and that you don't have to go through life as the victim.

Most people don't work on their dreams. The reason is usually fear: fear of failure, fear of success. Face your fears. Put yourself in a position where you must.

And when you don't feel worthy, take risks on yourself anyway. Most people are not risk takers. They earn a living, raise a family, and die. Believe in yourself.

> Someone's opinion of you does not have to become your reality.
