Logos and Iconography

The brand favors simple, theme-aware marks and text glyphs over a heavy icon font. The adopted logo is the green arch × Vestaboard flap; below it is the full exploration that led there, with the chosen direction highlighted.

The logo (shipping)​

The site's mark is the green cathedral arch housing a real Vestaboard split-flap that rolls B → 0 → 1 → blank (the byte, lit inside the faith frame). It is what you see in the navbar, and it is the same component demonstrated here:

The binary-pyramid mark remains the favicon / secondary mark (columns of 1 and 0 digits radiating from a central pillar), and the lightbulb </> mark (a bulb with a code glyph) is kept as an alternate. Both are theme-aware and inherit their color from the surrounding text:

The logo exploration​

Three ideas keep recurring: the lightbulb (purpose, the lit idea), binary (a byte is 8 bits), and code brackets </>. The same DNA was pushed in many directions. Each is shown live; the chosen directions are ringed.

Green arch × Vestaboard (the chosen direction)​

The one that won: the green cathedral arch as the housing for the brand's real Vestaboard split-flap, the faith frame around a mechanically rolling bit.

The green cathedral arch IS the housing for a Vestaboard flap that rolls B → 0 → 1 → blank. The brand's actual split-flap, set into the faith frame. Now the site's logo.

Arch + Flap (outline)
Same pairing as a hairline arch over a tinted interior; the dark flap pops. Lighter weight for favicons and dark surfaces.

A mono cursor inside the arch flashing 1 → 0 → empty. The byte, lit inside the faith frame, and it is alive.

'BoP' on a three-tile flap board hung under a green arch crown: the homepage departure-board sign, distilled into a mark.
✦ Our choice: Green arch × Vestaboard

This is now the site's adopted logo (look at the navbar). It won because it fuses the two strongest brand motifs into one living mark: the cathedral-arch silhouette (faith, and the same frame as the arch illustrations) becomes the physical housing for the Vestaboard split-flap that already runs on the homepage hero. It is ownable, it animates natively (the flap rolls B → 0 → 1 → blank, spelling the "byte"), and it ties the logo directly to the site's signature device. In the navbar it rests on "B" and rolls a quiet burst occasionally (or on hover); here it rolls continuously. Both honor reduced motion (the flap cross-fades instead of spinning).

Feature icons​

A small set of line-style SVGs used on the homepage feature cards, tinted brand-green. They are the only bundled icon set; when a UI needs a broader library, substitute Lucide (a thin, rounded line style that matches these) and flag the substitution.

Glyphs and emoji​

The brand reaches for text glyphs as icons before an icon font:

  • → on a "Read more" link.
  • ★ on premium surfaces.
  • ☕ the "Buy Me a Coffee?" cup in the navbar.
  • · the middot, the brand's connective separator in eyebrows, metadata, and footers.

Emoji appear only as the single leading glyph on a navigation card or a section header, never mid-sentence and never as decorative clutter. On this blog, the leading emoji on a post title also encodes the kind of post; the full legend lives in the Handbook.
