📘 Handbook: How This Site Works

This is the handbook for navigating the blog, the front door and the legend for the whole map. It explains the one idea the site is organized around (durable vs temporal), what the little emoji next to each post title mean, the kinds of posts I write, and where to find the glossary. The sidebar holds the rest: the glossary, every terminology page, and the component reference.

Durable vs temporal: the one idea​

Everything here is split into two halves by a single question: does this stay true over time, or is it a dated thing I did?

  • Craft and Journey are DURABLE. Distilled learnings, frameworks, strategy, terminology, the way I see the world (Craft) and myself (Journey). Return-to knowledge. When something I learn holds up, it lands here.
  • Initiatives are TEMPORAL. The specific, dated things I actually did: experiments, project logs, posts. Each one links up to the durable Craft insight it informed.

So if you want the lasting idea, read Craft or Journey. If you want what I actually did and when, read Initiatives. A temporal post is the evidence; a durable page is the lesson.

Thoughts, and the three ways one can graduate​

The temporal half starts with a Thought: an idea that occurred to me but I have not acted on. A thing I might build, a hypothetical I am playing out, a bet I am making, a critique that came to mind. A thought is a candidate, not a commitment, and it can graduate three ways depending on what I do with it:

  • Act on it → an Initiative. The moment I start doing something about an idea, it becomes a dated Initiative (an experiment, a project log).
  • Keep it to shape how I think → Mindset. The inputs I deliberately curate to influence my thinking: the quotes that moved me, the affirmations I return to, the principles I live by.
  • Distill it into a lasting lesson → Craft. When a thought holds up over time and generalizes, the durable lesson lands in Craft (or Journey).

Alongside these is a fourth temporal collection: Questions, the important sets of questions I ask. Some are introspective (the questions I ask myself to shape who I am), some are practical (worth asking before starting something). They are their own thing, not all of them mindset-shaping, so they live in their own collection.

The line between a Thought and a Mindset post is occurrence vs. curation. A quote that just occurred to me is a thought; keeping it because it moves me is Mindset. The Ideas board indexes the unactioned ideas, the same way the Experiments board indexes experiments: the durable board lives in Craft, the dated posts live on a blog.

The emoji tell you the TYPE of post​

Every post leads with an emoji in the Posts list. The emoji is not decoration and it is not about the topic. It tells you what KIND of thing the post is, so you can scan the list and find the format you are in the mood for. Here is the legend:

EmojiKindWhat it is
💡IdeaAn unactioned idea: something I might build or do, captured before I act on it. A Thought.
🔬ResearchSomething I want to research or learn before deciding. Carded on the Research board. A Thought.
🔮SimulationA hypothetical walk-through: if X then Y then Z, what if 1, 2, 3. A Thought.
🎯PredictionA falsifiable bet about the future: I call one outcome so I can check it later. A Thought.
🔍CritiqueAn evaluation of something that exists: what's wrong with it, or how it really works. A Thought.
❓Question setA set of questions I ask, introspective or practical. The "What I Ask Myself" / "Questions for" series. Lives in Questions.
💬Quote setA themed set of quotes that moved me, kept to shape my thinking. A Mindset input.
🪞PrincipleAn observation I keep as a rule that shapes me. A Mindset input (and the feeder for a durable Craft lesson).
🧩FrameworkA reusable model for thinking about something. "A Framework for", "The Anatomy of".
💭ReflectionA personal essay or set of takeaways. "Notes From", "What X Taught Me", affirmations.
🏗️System designAn architecture or build write-up: how something was designed and shipped.
📐Design storyThe story behind a design: why it exists, with a link to the full design doc.
🧪TutorialA hands-on, learn-by-doing walkthrough. "A Hands-On Way to", "Running X Locally".
📖ReferenceA concept explainer or taxonomy. "A Taxonomy of", "What Does X Mean".
🎛️ShowcaseA live showcase of one blog component/technique, with a list of posts that use it. The Components reference.
🎙️Event recapNotes from a conference or event.
🧭LegendA guide like this one: it indexes a set of posts and the conventions they share.
📚Learning planA sequenced plan for learning a topic: the goal, an ordered curriculum, resources, and how you know you're done.
📝Experiment planThe plan half of an experiment: the hypothesis and the design, before the result is in.
📊Experiment resultThe result half of an experiment: the outcome and the decision, once the data is in.

The title in the sidebar is kept short on purpose. The full title still lives at the top of each post and in the browser tab.

Where to start​

A few entry points depending on what you came for:

  • To sit with a question about yourself, start with the question sets (❓). The keystone is What I Ask Myself, which explains the practice and the power icons on each question.
  • To borrow a way of thinking, browse the frameworks (🧩).
  • To see how something was built, read the system designs (🏗️) and tutorials (🧪).
  • For a shorter, more personal read, the reflections (💭) are the place.

The glossary and terminology​

When a post uses a term of art, it is defined here in the Handbook. The sidebar holds:

  • The Glossary: the single A-to-Z index of every term, linking each to its definition.
  • Terminology: the per-topic term pages (software development, productivity, companies), plus the emoji and acronym conventions.

How the question sets work​

The largest group on the blog is the question sets. Each one is a themed collection of questions I actually return to, with a power icon on every question showing what kind of charge it carries (a jolt, a burn, something that reshapes you over time). The full legend for those power icons lives in the keystone post, What I Ask Myself. If you only read one thing, read that.
